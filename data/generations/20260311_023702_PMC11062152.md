## Summary

## Background
This retrospective single-center study evaluated 36 Japanese patients with metastatic pancreatic cancer treated with nanoliposomal irinotecan plus 5-fluorouracil and L-leucovorin (nal-IRI/FL). The main pharmacogenomic question was whether **UGT1A1** variants—specifically **UGT1A1*6** and **UGT1A1*28**—were associated with treatment-related toxicity. Patients were grouped as wild-type (−/−), heterozygous (+/−), or homozygous/compound heterozygous (+/+), and adverse events were assessed from medical records using CTCAE v5.0.

## Key Findings
The clearest pharmacogenomic signal was for **UGT1A1*6/*6, *28/*28, or *6/*28** grouped together as **UGT1A1*6 or *28 (+/+)**. These patients had significantly lower blood count nadirs during nal-IRI/FL treatment than patients with wild-type or single-variant genotypes. Median white blood cell nadirs were **2,050/mm³** in the (+/+) group versus **3,340/mm³** in wild-type and **3,220/mm³** in heterozygotes (**p=0.033**). Median neutrophil nadirs were **860/mm³** in the (+/+) group versus **1,930/mm³** and **1,285/mm³**, respectively (**p=0.043**). In multiple regression, the **UGT1A1*6 or *28 (+/+) genotype** remained significantly associated with greater white blood cell reduction (**p=0.009**) and neutrophil reduction (**p=0.017**).

Patients with **UGT1A1*6 or *28 (+/+)** also started nal-IRI at lower doses than other genotype groups, with a median initial dose of **48 mg/m²** versus **56 mg/m²** in the other groups (**p=0.011**). Although grade 3/4 leukopenia and neutropenia were numerically more common in the (+/+) group (**50.0%** vs **7.1%** and **12.5%** in the other groups), these differences were **not statistically significant** (**p=0.085** for both). **UGT1A1 genotype was not associated with diarrhea**: grade ≥1 diarrhea occurred in **27.3%**, **21.4%**, and **25.0%** of the wild-type, heterozygous, and (+/+) groups, and multivariable analysis found no significant genetic predictors. Overall, the study supports **UGT1A1*6/*6, *28/*28, and *6/*28** as markers of higher risk for nal-IRI/FL-related leukopenia and neutropenia.
