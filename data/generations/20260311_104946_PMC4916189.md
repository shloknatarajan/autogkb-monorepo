## Summary

## Background
This study evaluated whether genetic differences help explain efavirenz response in treatment-naïve adults with HIV receiving once-daily efavirenz 400 mg or 600 mg in the ENCORE1 trial through 96 weeks. The pharmacogenetic analysis examined variants in **CYP2B6, CYP2A6, CYP3A4, NR1I3, NR1I2,** and **ABCB1** for associations with virologic suppression, adverse events, and treatment discontinuation.

## Key Findings
- **Virologic response:** Genetic variation did **not** predict virologic success at 96 weeks.
  - **CYP2B6 516G>T (rs3745274):** No association with HIV RNA <200 copies/mL at week 96 (**p = 0.420**).
  - **CYP2B6 983T>C (rs28399499):** No association with virologic suppression (**p = 1.000**).
  - **CYP2B6 15582C>T (rs4803419):** No association with virologic suppression (**p = 1.000**).
  - **CYP2A6*9B, CYP2A6*17, CYP3A4*22, NR1I3 540C>T, NR1I3 1089T>C, ABCB1 3435C>T, NR1I2 63396C>T, NR1I2 7635A>G:** None were associated with HIV RNA <200 copies/mL at 96 weeks (all non-significant in Table 3).

- **Central nervous system (CNS) adverse events:** Several variants were associated with risk of CNS side effects on efavirenz.
  - **CYP2B6 983T>C (rs28399499):** Carriers of **TC/CC** had a **lower** risk of CNS adverse events than **TT** carriers; multivariable OR **0.30** (95% CI **0.12–0.75**), **p = 0.010**.
  - **CYP2B6 15582C>T (rs4803419):** Carriers of **CT/TT** had a **higher** risk of CNS adverse events than **CC** carriers; multivariable OR **1.59** (95% CI **1.11–2.27**), **p = 0.011**.
  - **ABCB1 3435C>T (rs1045642):** **TT** genotype was linked to a **higher** risk of CNS adverse events; multivariable OR **2.14** (95% CI **1.25–3.67**), **p = 0.006**.

- **Efavirenz discontinuation:** Some metabolism-related variants were associated with stopping efavirenz treatment.
  - **CYP2B6 516G>T (rs3745274):**
    - **GT vs GG:** higher risk of overall discontinuation; HR **1.80** (95% CI **1.01–3.21**), **p = 0.047**.
    - **TT vs GG:** higher risk of overall discontinuation; HR **2.66** (95% CI **1.26–5.60**), **p = 0.010**.
  - **CYP2A6*9B (rs8192726):** **CA/AA** carriers had higher risk of overall discontinuation; HR **2.00** (95% CI **1.14–3.52**), **p = 0.016**.
  - **NR1I2 63396C>T (rs2472677):** **TT** genotype was associated with a **lower** risk of overall discontinuation; HR **0.22** (95% CI **0.07–0.67**), **p = 0.008**.

- **Variants without clear safety/discontinuation associations:** Several tested polymorphisms were not significantly associated with key outcomes.
  - **CYP2A6*17 (rs28399454):** No significant association with virologic suppression or overall discontinuation (HR **0.55**, 95% CI **0.20–1.50**, **p = 0.240**).
  - **CYP3A4*22 (rs35599367):** No significant association with virologic suppression or overall discontinuation (HR **1.65**, 95% CI **0.66–4.10**, **p = 0.284**).
  - **NR1I2 7635A>G (rs6785049):** No significant association with virologic suppression or overall discontinuation.
  - **NR1I3 540C>T and 1089T>C:** No significant association with virologic suppression in this 96-week analysis.

- **Overall clinical takeaway:** The higher adverse-event burden seen with **efavirenz 600 mg** was **not explained by the tested polymorphisms**. The strongest pharmacogenomic signals in this study related to **CNS toxicity** and **treatment discontinuation**, not to antiviral efficacy.
