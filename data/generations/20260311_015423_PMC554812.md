## Summary

## Background
This case-control pharmacogenomic study examined why some Han Chinese patients in Taiwan develop severe cutaneous adverse reactions (SCAR) after starting allopurinol, including hypersensitivity syndrome (HSS), Stevens–Johnson syndrome (SJS), and toxic epidermal necrolysis (TEN). The investigators studied 51 patients with allopurinol-induced SCAR, 135 allopurinol-tolerant patients, and 93 healthy population controls. They first screened 823 SNPs in immune-related and drug metabolism genes, then performed high-resolution HLA typing.

## Key Findings
The strongest and clearest pharmacogenomic signal was **HLA-B*58:01**. This allele was found in **all 51 SCAR cases (100%)**, compared with **20/135 allopurinol-tolerant controls (15%)** and **19/93 population controls (20%)**. The association was extremely strong: versus tolerant controls, **OR 580.3 (95% CI 34.4–9780.9), corrected P = 4.7 × 10^-24**; versus population controls, **OR 393.5 (95% CI 23.2–6665.3), corrected P = 8.1 × 10^-18**. Other HLA alleles were also enriched in SCAR cases, including **HLA-C*03:02** (94% in cases; **OR 97.7**, corrected P = 1.4 × 10^-19 vs tolerant controls), **HLA-A*33:03** (67% in cases; **OR 9.3**, corrected P = 2.2 × 10^-4), and **HLA-DRB1*03:01** (65% in cases; **OR 12.7**, corrected P = 2.8 × 10^-6). These alleles were reported to be in linkage disequilibrium with HLA-B*58:01, forming an extended haplotype.

In the initial SNP screen, **29 SNPs in the MHC region** were associated with allopurinol-SCAR at **P < 0.01**. The most significant were **rs3117583 (BAT3)**, **rs1150793 (MSH5)**, and **rs2855804 (MICB)**, each with **P < 10^-7** in the discovery screen. Outside the MHC region, **rs2268791** and **rs1594** in **CFLAR** showed potential association and remained positive in replication, while **rs2304224** in **KIR2DL1** did not replicate. Notably, the study found **no significant associations among SNPs in genes involved in allopurinol metabolism**, suggesting the main signal came from immune-related HLA/MHC variation rather than drug-metabolizing enzyme variants.
