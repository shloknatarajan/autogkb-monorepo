# Effect of NLRP3 inflammasome genes polymorphism on disease susceptibility and response to TNF-α inhibitors in Iraqi patients with rheumatoid arthritis

## Summary

## Background
This case-control study evaluated whether two inflammasome-related variants—**CARD8 rs2043211** and **NLRP3 rs4612666**—are associated with rheumatoid arthritis (RA) outcomes and response to **TNF-α inhibitors** (**etanercept** or **infliximab**) in **100 Iraqi patients with RA** and **100 healthy controls**. Genotyping was performed by SYBR Green-based real-time PCR, and treatment response was assessed after 3 months using **DAS-28/EULAR-based criteria**.

## Key Findings
- **Disease susceptibility:** Neither variant was clearly associated with the risk of having RA in this cohort.
  - **CARD8 rs2043211 (A>T):** Genotype frequencies did not differ significantly between RA cases and controls (**P = 0.17**); allele frequencies were also not significantly different (**P = 0.059**).
  - **NLRP3 rs4612666 (C>T):** No significant difference in genotype distribution (**P = 0.08**) or allele frequencies (**P = 0.879**) between RA cases and controls.

- **Response to TNF-α inhibitors:** Variant genotypes were associated with poorer response to anti-TNF therapy after 3 months.
  - **CARD8 rs2043211:** Response differed significantly by genotype (**P < 0.0001**). The **TT genotype** was strongly enriched in non-responders (**15 non-responders vs 1 responder**), while **AA** was more common in responders (**32 responders vs 5 non-responders**). In genetic model analysis, **AA+AT** was associated with better response than **TT** (**P < 0.0001; OR 0.019, 95% CI 0.002–0.157**), while **AT+TT** was associated with lower response than **AA** (**P = 0.001; OR 0.183, 95% CI 0.063–0.531**).
  - **NLRP3 rs4612666:** Response also differed significantly by genotype (**P < 0.0001**). The **TT genotype** was more common in non-responders (**10 non-responders vs 1 responder**), while **CC** was more common in responders (**41 responders vs 6 non-responders**). In model analysis, **CC+CT** was associated with better response than **TT** (**P < 0.0001; OR 0.037, 95% CI 0.004–0.304**), while **CT+TT** was associated with lower response than **CC** (**P < 0.0001; OR 0.131, 95% CI 0.047–0.36**).

- **Disease activity after treatment:** The same variants associated with poor drug response also tracked with higher residual disease activity after TNF inhibitor treatment.
  - **CARD8 rs2043211:** Mean DAS-28 increased across genotypes **AA 3.51**, **AT 3.92**, **TT 4.65** (**P < 0.0001**). Carriers of **AT+TT** had higher disease activity than **AA** (**P = 0.029**).
  - **NLRP3 rs4612666:** Mean DAS-28 increased across genotypes **CC 3.52**, **CT 4.08**, **TT 4.72** (**P < 0.0001**). Carriers of **CT+TT** had higher disease activity than **CC** (**P = 0.002**).

- **Biomarker context (TNF-α and IL-1β):** Variant genotypes were linked to higher inflammatory cytokine levels after treatment, consistent with poorer treatment control.
  - **CARD8 rs2043211:** Cytokine levels rose markedly across genotypes: **TNF-α** was **5.64 pg/mL (AA)**, **7.39 pg/mL (AT)**, **14.25 pg/mL (TT)** and **IL-1β** was **1.05 pg/mL (AA)**, **2.25 pg/mL (AT)**, **4.55 pg/mL (TT)** (**P < 0.0001** for both). **AA+AT** was associated with lower TNF-α and IL-1β than **TT** (**P < 0.0001** for both).
  - **NLRP3 rs4612666:** Cytokine levels also increased across genotypes: **TNF-α** was **5.56 pg/mL (CC)**, **8.58 pg/mL (CT)**, **14.74 pg/mL (TT)** and **IL-1β** was **1.18 pg/mL (CC)**, **2.59 pg/mL (CT)**, **4.86 pg/mL (TT)** (**P < 0.0001** for both). **CT+TT** was associated with higher TNF-α (**P = 0.002**) and IL-1β (**P < 0.0001**) than **CC**.

- **Non-genetic treatment response markers:** Higher cytokine levels were also associated with poorer TNF inhibitor response overall.
  - **TNF-α:** Higher in non-responders than responders (**13.68 vs 4.83 pg/mL, P < 0.0001**) and correlated with DAS-28 (**r² = 0.45, P < 0.0001**).
  - **IL-1β:** Higher in non-responders than responders (**3.36 vs 1.57 pg/mL, P < 0.0001**) and correlated with DAS-28 (**r² = 0.51, P < 0.0001**).

Overall, in this Iraqi RA cohort, **CARD8 rs2043211 T-bearing genotypes** and **NLRP3 rs4612666 T-bearing genotypes** were **not associated with RA susceptibility**, but were associated with **worse response to etanercept/infliximab, higher post-treatment disease activity, and higher TNF-α and IL-1β levels**.
