# Shared peptide binding of HLA Class I and II alleles associate with cutaneous nevirapine hypersensitivity and identify novel risk alleles

**PMCID:** PMC5561238  
**PMID:** 28819312

## Summary

## Background
This study examined pharmacogenomic predictors of **severe cutaneous nevirapine hypersensitivity** (grade 3/4 rash) in HIV-1–infected adults. The analysis included **151 cases and 413 nevirapine-tolerant controls** with high-resolution HLA typing, and used logistic regression adjusted for ancestry. The main focus was on how **shared HLA peptide-binding pocket features** relate to risk or protection.

## Key Findings
- **Primary genetic risk signal: HLA-C alleles linked to nevirapine rash**
  - **HLA-C*04:01:** Strongest and most consistent risk allele across ancestry groups.
    - Whole cohort: **OR 3.06, p=0.0001**
    - Asian participants: **OR 5.49, p=0.0001**
    - Caucasian participants: **OR 2.08, p=0.02**
    - African participants: **OR 3.84, p=0.04**
    - In multivariable analysis: **OR 4.06 (95% CI 2.39–6.88)**
  - **Shared HLA-C F-pocket risk cluster:** Alleles sharing the HLA-C*04:01 F-pocket motif were associated with increased risk, supporting a class effect rather than a single-allele effect.
    - Cluster included **HLA-C*04:03, *04:06, *04:07, *05:01, *05:09, *18:01**
    - Multivariable OR for the non-*04:01* F-pocket risk cluster: **2.91 (95% CI 1.62–5.23)**
  - **HLA-C*05:01:** Risk allele, especially in Caucasians.
    - Caucasians: **OR 2.84, p=0.002**
    - Whole-cohort cluster analysis: **OR 2.67, p=0.002**
  - **HLA-C*18:01:** Suggested risk in Africans, though evidence was weaker.
    - Africans: **OR 2.67, p=0.2**
    - Compared with non-*C*04:01/*C*18:01 carriers: **OR 4.71, p=0.06**

- **Protective HLA-B effects: lower risk of cutaneous hypersensitivity**
  - **Protective HLA-B B62/B-pocket cluster:** A group of alleles sharing a common HLA-B B-pocket motif was associated with protection.
    - Overall cluster: **OR 0.18 (95% CI 0.07–0.46)**
    - Included **HLA-B*15:01, *15:12, *15:24, *15:25, *15:27, *15:32, *15:35, and HLA-B*52:01**
  - **HLA-B*15 group:** Protective in supertype analysis.
    - Adjusted for race only: **OR 0.43 (95% CI 0.25–0.74), p=0.002**
    - Adjusted for race and HLA-C risk cluster: **OR 0.40 (95% CI 0.23–0.70), p=0.001**
  - **HLA-B*52:01:** Included in the protective cluster, though the 2-digit B*52 group alone was not clearly significant in Table 1.

- **Secondary HLA-B risk alleles/clusters: additional susceptibility beyond HLA-C**
  - **HLA-B*35:05:** Most prominent single HLA-B risk allele; uniquely carried the risk E-pocket sequence identified in the study.
    - Included in multivariable risk cluster with **OR 2.32 (95% CI 1.42–3.79)**
  - **B07-related HLA-B risk cluster:** After accounting for the HLA-C*04:01 risk group, several alleles were associated with increased risk.
    - Included **HLA-B*51:01, *51:02, *54:01, *55:01, *55:02, *56:01, *56:04, *67:01, *78:01, and HLA-B*39:10**
    - Cluster OR: **2.32 (95% CI 1.42–3.79)**
    - Related 2-digit grouping (**B*51/*54/*55/*56**) after adjustment: **OR 2.09 (95% CI 1.29–3.39), p=0.003**
  - **Non-B07 HLA-B risk cluster:** Additional increased-risk alleles.
    - Included **HLA-B*13:02, *38:01, *38:02, *39:01, *39:05, *39:06, *39:09, *51:07**
    - Cluster OR: **1.76 (95% CI 1.02–3.02)**
  - **HLA-B*38/*39 group:** Significant after adjustment for the HLA-C risk cluster.
    - **OR 1.82 (95% CI 1.02–3.22), p=0.04**

- **Secondary HLA class II risk: HLA-DRB1 P4-pocket cluster**
  - **Risk HLA-DRB1 cluster:** A class II cluster sharing a common P4-pocket sequence was associated with increased risk of cutaneous nevirapine hypersensitivity.
    - Included **HLA-DRB1*01:01, *01:02, *01:03, *04:04, *04:05, *04:08, *04:10**
    - Overall multivariable OR: **2.00 (95% CI 1.23–3.24)**
    - Stronger in Asians: **OR 3.23 (95% CI 1.40–7.43)**
  - **HLA-DRB1*04:01 and HLA-DRB1*04:15:** In contrast, these alleles appeared protective in Caucasians.
    - **2 cases vs 26 controls, p=0.01**

- **Non-HLA metabolism factor retained in the multivariable model**
  - **CYP2B6 slow metabolizer genotype:** Independently associated with greater risk of cutaneous nevirapine hypersensitivity.
    - **OR 2.07 (95% CI 1.12–3.82)**

- **Functional follow-up relevant to biomarker interpretation**
  - **HLA-DRB1*01:01 peptide elution/binding studies:** In cell-based peptide elution and binding assays, nevirapine did **not** measurably alter peptide binding to HLA-DRB1*01:01.
    - 605 peptides were eluted from untreated cells vs 359 from nevirapine-treated cells, but no significant peptide-anchor differences remained after multiple-comparison adjustment.
    - Direct peptide-binding assays showed **no difference** in binding to HLA-DRB1*01:01 in the presence vs absence of nevirapine.

Overall, the strongest pharmacogenomic predictor of severe cutaneous nevirapine hypersensitivity was **HLA-C*04:01**, with risk extending to other **HLA-C alleles sharing the same F-pocket motif**. Additional **HLA-B risk and protective clusters**, a **HLA-DRB1 risk cluster**, and **CYP2B6 slow metabolizer status** further modified risk.
