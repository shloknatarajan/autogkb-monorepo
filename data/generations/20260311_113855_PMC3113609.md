## Summary

## Background
This study examined whether genetic variation in the HLA region influences the risk of carbamazepine-induced hypersensitivity reactions in people of European ancestry. The investigators used genomewide association analyses, HLA imputation, and confirmatory high-resolution HLA typing in patients with carbamazepine-induced hypersensitivity syndrome, maculopapular exanthema, and Stevens–Johnson syndrome/toxic epidermal necrolysis (SJS–TEN), compared with population and carbamazepine-tolerant clinical controls.

## Key Findings
- **Primary risk allele for carbamazepine hypersensitivity in Europeans:** The main pharmacogenomic signal was in the HLA-A region, specifically HLA-A*31:01.
  - **HLA-A*31:01:** Strongly associated with carbamazepine-induced hypersensitivity syndrome in the genomewide analysis (P = 3.5×10^-8^). The allele was present in 40.0% of hypersensitivity syndrome cases versus 4.9% of controls.
  - **rs1061235:** The top associated SNP in the GWAS; it served as a proxy for **HLA-A*31:01** and was in complete linkage disequilibrium with it in Europeans (r^2^ = 1). High-resolution HLA typing showed 100% concordance with imputed alleles.

- **Association across multiple clinical phenotypes:** The HLA-A*31:01 signal was not limited to one type of reaction.
  - **HLA-A*31:01 / rs1061235:** In follow-up analysis using clinical carbamazepine-tolerant controls, the allele was associated with hypersensitivity syndrome/AGEP, with an odds ratio (OR) of 12.41 (95% CI, 1.27–121.03; P = 0.03).
  - **HLA-A*31:01 / rs1061235:** For maculopapular exanthema, the initial GWAS showed the strongest association at this allele (P = 1.11×10^-6^), with 27% of cases versus 4% of controls carrying it; combined analysis gave OR 8.33 (95% CI, 3.59–19.36; P = 8.0×10^-7^).
  - **HLA-A*31:01 / rs1061235:** For SJS–TEN, 42% of cases carried the allele versus 4% of carbamazepine-tolerant controls, with OR 25.93 (95% CI, 4.93–116.18; P = 8.0×10^-5^).

- **Overall predictive value for carbamazepine hypersensitivity:** The variant increased risk across the full hypersensitivity spectrum, although it was not highly sensitive.
  - **HLA-A*31:01 / rs1061235:** In pooled analysis of all 145 hypersensitivity cases versus 257 carbamazepine-tolerant controls, the association remained strong: OR 9.12 (95% CI, 4.03–20.65; P = 1.0×10^-7^).
  - **HLA-A*31:01:** Test characteristics from the pooled dataset were sensitivity 26% and specificity 96%. Assuming a baseline hypersensitivity risk of 5%, carrying the allele increased estimated risk to 26.0%, while not carrying it lowered risk to 3.8%.

- **Biomarker validation context:** The study supports HLA-A*31:01 as the relevant marker rather than another nearby HLA-A allele.
  - **HLA-A*31:01:** Confirmed by direct high-resolution HLA typing after imputation from GWAS data.
  - **Other HLA-A alleles:** No other classic HLA-A alleles reached nominal significance in the hypersensitivity syndrome analysis.
