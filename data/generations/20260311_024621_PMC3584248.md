# CYP2D6 genotypes, endoxifen levels, and disease recurrence in 224 Filipino and Vietnamese women receiving adjuvant tamoxifen for operable breast cancer

## Summary

## Background
This study evaluated whether **CYP2D6 genetic variation** affects tamoxifen metabolism in **224 Filipino and Vietnamese women** receiving adjuvant **tamoxifen 20 mg/day** for operable hormone receptor–positive breast cancer. Investigators genotyped common CYP2D6 alleles relevant to these populations (**\*2, \*3, \*4, \*5, \*6, \*10, \*41**) and measured plasma tamoxifen metabolites after at least 4 months of therapy, when steady-state levels were expected. They also performed a nested case–control analysis in **48 women** (24 with recurrence, 24 without) to explore whether endoxifen levels were related to recurrence.

## Key Findings
**CYP2D6 genotype was significantly associated with endoxifen exposure**, the main active tamoxifen metabolite. Patients were grouped as **normal metabolizers** (functional alleles **\*1 or \*2**), **intermediate metabolizers** (reduced-function genotypes involving **\*10 or \*41**), and **slow metabolizers** (for example **\*10/\*10, \*10/\*41**, or genotypes including null alleles such as **\*5**). Endoxifen concentrations decreased across these groups: **48.2 ± 39.7 ng/mL** in normal metabolizers, **39.3 ± 26.6 ng/mL** in intermediate metabolizers, and **25.0 ± 22.3 ng/mL** in slow metabolizers (**p < 0.0001 for trend**; Figure 1 reported **p = 0.0001**). However, **CYP2D6 explained only about 8.6% of the variability** in endoxifen levels, indicating that genotype affected metabolism but did not account for most between-patient differences.

At the variant level, **reduced-function or nonfunctional alleles**—especially **CYP2D6 \*10, \*41, and \*5** in combinations such as **\*1/\*10, \*2/\*10, \*10/\*10, \*10/\*41, \*1/\*41, \*1/\*5, \*2/\*5, and \*5/\*10**—were associated with **lower endoxifen concentrations** than fully functional genotypes. In contrast, genotypes containing **functional alleles \*1 and \*2** (for example **\*1/\*1, \*1/\*2, \*2/\*2**) were associated with **higher endoxifen levels**. CYP2D6 status also showed a weaker association with **4-hydroxytamoxifen** levels (**p = 0.13**, variance explained **1.0%**) and was not clearly associated with parent tamoxifen levels.

In the recurrence analysis, the study did **not** find a simple relationship in which lower-CYP2D6 activity predicted recurrence. Instead, recurrence risk was associated with **endoxifen concentration itself** in a **quadratic pattern** in the matched Cox model (**likelihood ratio p = 0.002**). The paper reports that among women with **low endoxifen** levels, **8 of 9** recurrent cases had lower levels than their matched controls, while **higher endoxifen concentrations (>70 ng/mL)** were also seen in a subset of recurrent cases. These outcome findings were exploratory and based on the metabolite level analysis rather than a direct genotype–recurrence association.
