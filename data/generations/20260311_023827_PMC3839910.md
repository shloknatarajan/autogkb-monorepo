# HLA-A*31:01 and HLA-B*15:02 as genetic markers for carbamazepine hypersensitivity in children

## Summary

## Background
This study examined whether two HLA risk alleles, **HLA-A*31:01** and **HLA-B*15:02**, predict **carbamazepine (CBZ) hypersensitivity** in children. Investigators genotyped **42 Canadian pediatric CBZ hypersensitivity cases** (including HSS, SJS/TEN, MPE, and 1 AGEP case) and **91 CBZ-tolerant controls**. **HLA-B*15:02** was assessed by direct typing, and **HLA-A*31:01** was assessed by both direct sequence-based typing and the proxy SNP **rs1061235**.

## Key Findings
**HLA-A*31:01** was strongly associated with **CBZ hypersensitivity syndrome (HSS)** and **maculopapular exanthema (MPE)**, but not with **SJS/TEN**. It was present in **3/6 HSS cases** versus **3/91 controls** (**OR 26.36, p=0.0025**) and in **6/26 MPE cases** (**OR 8.57, p=0.0037**). When HSS and MPE were combined, the association remained strong (**OR 11.18, p=2.6×10^-4**). Across **all CBZ hypersensitivity cases**, HLA-A*31:01 was also enriched (**9/42 cases; OR 7.85, p=0.0016**). In the subgroup with European ancestry, the association was similar: **all CBZ cases OR 7.62 (p=0.025)** and **HSS/MPE OR 10.05 (p=0.013)**.

In contrast, **HLA-B*15:02** was specifically associated with **CBZ-induced SJS/TEN**. It was found in **3/9 SJS/TEN cases** and showed a strong association with this severe reaction (**OR 38.65, p=0.0022**). None of the HSS or MPE cases carried HLA-B*15:02, and no significant association was seen for those phenotypes or for all CBZ hypersensitivity cases combined.

The proxy marker **rs1061235 T allele** also showed strong associations with **CBZ-HSS** (**OR 25.97, p=8.2×10^-4**), **CBZ-MPE** (**OR 7.32, p=7.4×10^-4**), combined **HSS/MPE** (**OR 9.45, p=2.6×10^-5**), and **all CBZ hypersensitivity** (**OR 6.96, p=1.5×10^-4**), but not with **SJS/TEN** (**OR 0.69, p=1.000**). However, the Results showed that rs1061235 was **not a perfect surrogate for HLA-A*31:01 in this multiethnic pediatric cohort**: 20 children carried rs1061235T, but only 12 also carried HLA-A*31:01 by direct typing.
