## Summary

## Background
This retrospective nested case-control study evaluated 82 Chilean patients with stage III/IV colorectal cancer treated with 5-fluorouracil (5-FU)-based chemotherapy regimens (including FOLFOX, CAPOX, FOLFIRI, capecitabine, and 5-FU/LV). The authors genotyped 16 variants in genes involved in fluoropyrimidine metabolism, folate biology, DNA repair, detoxification, and drug transport, and tested their associations with treatment-related adverse drug reactions using logistic regression.

## Key Findings
The clearest pharmacogenomic signals involved **GSTP1, DPYD, and TYMS**. The **GSTP1 rs1695 G allele** was associated with a **lower risk of neuropathy/nervous system toxicity** (neuropathy OR 0.147, p=0.012; ungrouped neuropathy OR 0.01, p=0.008; nervous system disorders G/G vs A/A OR 0.098, p=0.015), and also with **less hematologic toxicity** (dominant model OR 0.298, p=0.001). However, the same variant was linked to a **higher risk of mucositis** (OR 2.27, p=0.036). The **DPYD rs1801265 (c.85T>C)** variant was associated with **greater neuropathy risk**, with carriers of the C allele showing increased risk (dominant model OR 4.583, p=0.05; T/C vs T/T OR 30.0, p=0.0049). For **TYMS**, the **3′UTR 6-bp deletion rs11280056** was consistently **protective against hematologic toxicity** (INS/DEL vs INS/INS OR 0.156, p=0.002; DEL/DEL vs INS/INS OR 0.029, p=0.001; dominant model OR 0.107, p<0.0001), and also reduced **severe hematologic toxicity** (OR 0.098, p=0.005) and **gastrointestinal toxicity** (DEL/DEL vs INS/INS OR 0.100, p=0.045). In contrast, the **TYMS 5′UTR rs45445694 3R/3R genotype** was associated with **higher risk of skin/subcutaneous toxicity** (OR 6.40, p=0.029).

Other significant associations were seen for **MTHFR, TYMP, ABCB1, and ERCC2**. The **MTHFR rs1801131 C allele** was associated with **lower pain risk** (A/C vs A/A OR 0.355, p=0.042; dominant model OR 0.375, p=0.048), but **higher nervous system toxicity** (dominant model OR 5.805, p=0.035). The **TYMP rs11479 T allele** was linked to **higher pain risk** (dominant model OR 5.860, p=0.027), and **ABCB1 rs1128503 C/C** was also associated with **higher pain risk** (OR 4.200, p=0.049). **ERCC2 rs13181 C/C** showed a **lower risk of gastrointestinal toxicity** (OR 0.100, p=0.045). In multivariable analysis, a **pain prediction model** including **ERCC2 rs13181 C/C** (OR 6.272, p=0.034) and **MTHFR rs1801131 A/C+C/C** (OR 0.159, p=0.034) was significant (model p=0.01), and an **anemia model** including **TYMP rs11479 T/T** (OR 23.760, p=0.038) was also significant overall (model p=0.027), while **ABCB1 rs1045642 T/T** was not independently significant in that model (OR 0.153, p=0.093). Several other tested variants, including **DPYD rs3918290 and rs55886062**, showed no informative association in this cohort because they had no variation or no significant toxicity association.
