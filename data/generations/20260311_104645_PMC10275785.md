# Effect of NLRP3 inflammasome genes polymorphism on disease susceptibility and response to TNF-α inhibitors in Iraqi patients with rheumatoid arthritis

## Summary

## Background
This case-control study evaluated whether polymorphisms in inflammasome-related genes affect response to TNF-α inhibitors in 100 Iraqi patients with rheumatoid arthritis (RA) treated with etanercept or infliximab for at least 3 months. The two variants genotyped by real-time PCR were **CARD8 rs2043211 (A>T)** and **NLRP3 rs4612666 (C>T)**. Treatment response was assessed using DAS-28/EULAR-based criteria, and serum **TNF-α** and **IL-1β** were measured by ELISA.

## Key Findings
- **Disease susceptibility:** Neither tested variant was significantly associated with overall risk of having RA versus being a healthy control.
  - **CARD8 rs2043211:** No significant difference in genotype distribution between RA cases and controls (**P=0.17**) or allele frequencies (**P=0.059**).
  - **NLRP3 rs4612666:** No significant difference in genotype distribution (**P=0.08**) or allele frequencies (**P=0.879**) between cases and controls.

- **Treatment response to TNF-α inhibitors:** Variant genotypes were associated with poorer response to etanercept/infliximab after 3 months.
  - **CARD8 rs2043211:** Genotypes differed significantly between responders and non-responders (**P<0.0001**). The **TT genotype** was strongly enriched in non-responders (**15 non-responders vs 1 responder**), while **AA** was more common in responders (**32 responders vs 5 non-responders**).
  - **NLRP3 rs4612666:** Genotypes also differed significantly by response status (**P<0.0001**). The **TT genotype** was more frequent in non-responders (**10 non-responders vs 1 responder**), while **CC** was more common in responders (**41 responders vs 6 non-responders**).

- **Disease activity/severity during treatment:** The same variants were linked to higher DAS-28 scores and more active disease after 3 months of TNF-α inhibitor therapy.
  - **CARD8 rs2043211:** Mean DAS-28 increased across genotypes: **AA 3.51**, **AT 3.92**, **TT 4.65** (**P<0.0001**). The TT genotype was more frequent in patients with high disease activity.
  - **NLRP3 rs4612666:** Mean DAS-28 also increased across genotypes: **CC 3.52**, **CT 4.08**, **TT 4.72** (**P<0.0001**). TT carriers had the highest disease activity.

- **Biomarker context:** Variant genotypes were associated with higher inflammatory cytokine levels, which were also linked to poor treatment response.
  - **CARD8 rs2043211:** Cytokine levels rose across genotypes, with the **TT genotype** showing the highest levels: **TNF-α 14.25 pg/mL** and **IL-1β 4.55 pg/mL**, versus **AA** at **5.64 pg/mL** and **1.05 pg/mL** respectively (**P<0.0001** for both).
  - **NLRP3 rs4612666:** The **TT genotype** likewise had the highest cytokine levels: **TNF-α 14.74 pg/mL** and **IL-1β 4.86 pg/mL**, versus **CC** at **5.56 pg/mL** and **1.18 pg/mL** (**P<0.0001** for both).
  - **Clinical biomarker association:** Non-responders had substantially higher cytokine levels than responders: **TNF-α 13.68 vs 4.83 pg/mL** and **IL-1β 3.36 vs 1.57 pg/mL** (both **P<0.0001**). DAS-28 correlated with **TNF-α** (**r²=0.45, P<0.0001**) and **IL-1β** (**r²=0.51, P<0.0001**).

- **Genetic model results:** T-allele–bearing genotypes generally tracked with worse outcomes on TNF-α inhibitors.
  - **CARD8 rs2043211:** Genetic model analyses showed significant associations with response and disease activity, with T-allele–bearing groups linked to poorer response (**P=0.001 to <0.0001**, depending on model) and higher cytokine levels (**P<0.0001**).
  - **NLRP3 rs4612666:** T-allele–bearing groups were also associated with poorer response (**P<0.0001**) and higher DAS-28/cytokine levels, especially in recessive-model comparisons (**DAS-28 P<0.0001; TNF-α P=0.002; IL-1β P<0.0001**).

Overall, in this cohort, **CARD8 rs2043211 TT** and **NLRP3 rs4612666 TT** were the key pharmacogenomic markers linked to **lower response to TNF-α inhibitors**, **higher residual disease activity**, and **higher inflammatory cytokine levels**, while neither variant clearly predicted RA susceptibility.
