# Integrating rare genetic variants into DPYD pharmacogenetic testing may help preventing fluoropyrimidine-induced toxicity

**PMCID:** PMC10786722  
**PMID:** 38216550

## Summary

## Background
This study evaluated whether broad **DPYD** sequencing can better identify patients with reduced **DPD** enzyme activity before fluoropyrimidine treatment (such as 5-FU or capecitabine). The authors sequenced the coding regions and splice junctions of **DPYD** in **2,972 patients** eligible for fluoropyrimidine-based chemotherapy and compared genetic findings with a pretreatment DPD phenotype measured mainly by the **plasma dihydrouracil/uracil ([UH2]/[U]) ratio**. Partial DPD deficiency was defined as **[UH2]/[U] ≤ 10**.

## Key Findings
- **Established high-risk DPYD variants were strongly associated with reduced DPD activity:**
  - **DPYD c.1905+1G>A (rs3918290, *DPYD*2A):** Significantly associated with low DPD activity (**p < 0.001**).
  - **DPYD c.1679T>G (rs55886062, *DPYD*13):** Significantly associated with low DPD activity, with the strongest reported signal among the guideline variants (**p < 0.0001**).
  - **DPYD c.2846A>T (rs67376798, p.Asp949Val):** Significantly associated with low DPD activity (**p < 0.001**).

- **Some common DPYD variants showed more modest associations with reduced DPD activity:**
  - **DPYD c.1236G>A (rs56038477; part of hapB3):** Significantly associated with lower DPD activity (**p < 0.05**).
  - **DPYD c.496A>G (rs2297595, p.Met166Val):** Significantly associated with lower DPD activity (**p < 0.05**).
  - **DPYD c.2194G>A (rs1801160, *DPYD*6, p.Val732Ile):** Significantly associated with lower DPD activity (**p < 0.01**).
  - **Not significantly associated in this cohort:** rs17376848, rs1801159 (*DPYD*5), rs1801158 (*DPYD*4), and rs1801265 (*DPYD*9A).

- **Rare DPYD variants were enriched in patients with low DPD activity:**
  - **Any rare variant (MAF < 1%):** Found more often in patients with partial DPD deficiency than in those with normal DPD activity (**9.3% vs 3.2%; p < 0.00001**).
  - **Rare variants excluding the 3 established guideline-risk variants:** Still enriched in patients with low DPD activity (**4.5% vs 2.6%; p < 0.03**).
  - **Rare variants with predicted deleterious effect (MAF < 1% and CADD > 15), excluding established guideline-risk variants:** More common in patients with low DPD activity (**4.2% vs 1.6%; p < 0.001**).

- **Additional rare or very rare DPYD variants observed only or mainly in patients with low DPD activity:**
  - Variants seen in deficient patients and highlighted by the sequencing analysis included:
    - **rs141044036** (c.2872A>G, p.Lys958Glu)
    - **rs114096998** (c.3067C>A, p.Pro1023Thr)
    - **rs145548112** (c.2161G>A, p.Ala721Thr)
    - **rs45589337** (c.775A>G, p.Lys259Glu)
    - **rs61622928** (c.1218G>A, p.Met406Ile)
    - **rs539032572** (*DPYD*7; c.299_302del, frameshift)
    - **rs376073289** (c.623G>T, p.Arg208Leu)
    - **rs72549308** (c.601A>C, p.Ser201Arg)
    - **rs142619737** (c.1615G>A, p.Gly539Arg)
    - **rs368146607** (c.1846A>C, p.Lys616Gln)
    - **rs138616379** (c.1775G>A, p.Arg592Gln)
    - **rs147601618** (c.1796T>C, p.Met599Thr)
  - These rare variants were identified in heterozygous carriers and were associated with reduced DPD activity based on phenotype distribution, but most individual variants were too uncommon for robust single-variant statistical estimates.

- **Phenotype definition context relevant to testing:**
  - Using the study’s primary phenotype definition, **580/2,972 patients (19.7%)** had partial DPD deficiency by **[UH2]/[U] ≤ 10**.
  - Uracil concentration alone and the [UH2]/[U] ratio did **not** classify deficiency equivalently, supporting the use of genotype as a complementary tool in pretreatment assessment.

Overall, the results show that, in addition to the well-known actionable **DPYD** variants, **rare deleterious DPYD variants contribute meaningfully to reduced DPD activity**, suggesting that broader sequencing may improve pharmacogenetic risk assessment before fluoropyrimidine therapy.
