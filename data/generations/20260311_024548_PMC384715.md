## Summary

## Background
This study examined genetic predictors of **abacavir hypersensitivity** in 248 HIV-positive patients exposed to abacavir from the Western Australian HIV Cohort Study. Using strict clinical criteria, patch testing, and ex vivo immune assays, the authors identified **18 definite hypersensitivity cases** and **230 tolerant controls**, then tested MHC-region variants to determine which genetic markers were most strongly associated with this adverse drug reaction.

## Key Findings
The strongest single genetic association was with **HLA-B*57:01**. It was present in **17 of 18 hypersensitivity cases (94.4%)** versus **4 of 230 tolerant controls (1.7%)**, corresponding to an **odds ratio (OR) of 960** with **corrected p < 0.0001**. In the prospective cohort, no **HLA-B*57:01-negative** patient developed hypersensitivity (**0% incidence; 95% CI 0–0.075**), supporting its value as a negative predictor. Other markers on the ancestral haplotype were also associated with risk, including **HLA-DRB1*07:01/HLA-DQ3** (**77.8% vs 4.8%; OR 69.7; corrected p < 0.0001**) and **C4A6** (**77.8% vs 3.0%; OR 111.5; corrected p < 0.0001**), but these were weaker than HLA-B*57:01 alone.

Fine mapping narrowed the susceptibility region to the **Hsp70 gene cluster**, highlighting a nonsynonymous variant in **Hsp70-Hom (HspA1L), M493T**. This variant alone was found in **94.4% of cases** and **22.2% of controls** (**OR 59.7; corrected p < 0.00001**). However, the combination of **HLA-B*57:01 plus Hsp70-Hom M493T** showed the strongest association: **17 of 18 cases (94.4%)** versus **1 of 230 controls (0.43%)**, with an **OR of 3,893** and **corrected p < 0.00001**. This combination significantly improved discrimination between hypersensitive and tolerant patients compared with **HLA-B*57:01** alone (**p = 0.003**).

Functional testing supported an immune-mediated drug reaction: patients with abacavir hypersensitivity had increased **monocyte TNF expression** after ex vivo abacavir stimulation compared with tolerant controls (**p = 0.008**), and TNF secretion was reduced after **CD8+ T-cell depletion**. Overall, the results show that **HLA-B*57:01** is the main pharmacogenomic marker for abacavir hypersensitivity, with **Hsp70-Hom M493T** further refining risk prediction when present together with HLA-B*57:01.
