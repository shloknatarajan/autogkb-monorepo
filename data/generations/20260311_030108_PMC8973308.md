# Susceptibility to thiopurine toxicity by TPMT and NUDT15 variants in Colombian children with acute lymphoblastic leukemia

## Summary

## Background
This longitudinal observational study examined whether common thiopurine-related variants in **NUDT15** and **TPMT** were associated with toxicity from **6-mercaptopurine (6-MP)** during maintenance therapy in **Colombian children with acute lymphoblastic leukemia (ALL)**. Genotyping was performed by **TaqMan real-time PCR** for **NUDT15 rs116855232** and **TPMT rs1800462, rs1800460, and rs1142345**. Clinical outcomes were followed monthly for 6 months and included **myelotoxicity, liver toxicity, 6-MP dose reduction, and 6-MP interruption**; febrile neutropenia, relapse, and death were also explored.

## Key Findings
Among the 68 successfully genotyped patients, **heterozygous variant carriers were uncommon**: **4/68** for **NUDT15 rs116855232**, **4/68** for **TPMT rs1800462**, **6/68** for **TPMT rs1800460**, and **4/42** for **TPMT rs1142345**. No patients were homozygous for a variant allele. For the 42 patients with complete TPMT genotyping, inferred diplotypes were mostly **TPMT *1/*1** (36 patients), with smaller numbers of **TPMT *1/*2** (2 patients) and **TPMT *1/*3A** (4 patients).

**No statistically significant association** was found between any of the tested **NUDT15** or **TPMT** variants and the main treatment-toxicity outcomes, including **6-MP dose reduction, 6-MP interruption, myelotoxicity/febrile neutropenia, liver toxicity, relapse, or death** (chi-square analysis; significance threshold **p < 0.05**; no significant p-values reported). Likewise, **TPMT diplotype-based analysis** (*1/*1 vs *1/*2 or *1/*3A) showed **no significant association** with toxicity outcomes. Laboratory markers followed over 6 months (ALT, AST, bilirubin, leukocyte and differential counts) also **did not differ significantly by genotype**.

Although not statistically significant, the raw data showed some possible signals: **2 of 4** children with **NUDT15 rs116855232 C/T** required **6-MP dose reduction**, and **2 of 4** with **TPMT rs1800462 C/G** had **febrile neutropenia** and also required **6-MP interruption**. However, these observations did not reach statistical significance in this cohort.
