## Summary

## Background
This retrospective nested case-control study evaluated 82 Chilean patients with advanced colorectal cancer treated with fluoropyrimidine-based chemotherapy (mainly FOLFOX, CAPOX, FOLFIRI, capecitabine, or 5-FU/LV). The authors genotyped 16 variants in genes involved in 5-FU and oxaliplatin pharmacology and tested associations with chemotherapy-related adverse drug reactions using logistic regression.

## Key Findings
- **Neurologic toxicity (including neuropathy):** Several variants were linked to risk of nervous system adverse events during 5-FU–based treatment.
  - **GSTP1 rs1695 (c.313A>G):** The G allele was associated with lower risk of neuropathy and nervous system toxicity.
    - Neuropathy: G allele protective (reported OR = 0.147, p = 0.012); supplementary analysis also showed OR = 0.01, p = 0.008.
    - Nervous system disorders: G/G genotype vs A/A, OR = 0.098, p = 0.015; recessive model (G/G vs A/A+A/G), OR = 0.182, p = 0.020.
  - **DPYD rs1801265 (c.85T>C, DPYD*9):** The C allele was associated with higher neuropathy risk.
    - Dominant model: OR = 4.583, p = 0.05.
    - T/C genotype vs T/T: OR = 30.0, p = 0.0049.
  - **MTHFR rs1801131 (c.1298A>C):** Carriers of the C allele had higher risk of nervous system disorders.
    - Dominant model (A/C + C/C vs A/A): OR = 5.805, p = 0.035.

- **Hematologic toxicity:** The strongest protective associations were seen for TYMS and GSTP1.
  - **TYMS rs11280056 (3'UTR 6-bp ins/del):** The deletion allele was associated with lower risk of hematologic toxicity.
    - INS/DEL vs INS/INS: OR = 0.156, p = 0.002.
    - DEL/DEL vs INS/INS: OR = 0.029, p = 0.001.
    - Dominant model (INS/DEL + DEL/DEL vs INS/INS): OR = 0.107, p < 0.0001.
    - Severe hematologic toxicity (grade 3–4): dominant model OR = 0.098, p = 0.005.
  - **GSTP1 rs1695:** Also associated with lower risk of hematologic disorders.
    - Dominant model (A/G + G/G vs A/A): OR = 0.298, p = 0.001.
    - Recessive model (G/G vs A/A + A/G): OR = 0.157, p = 0.0005.

- **Gastrointestinal and mucosal toxicity:** Different variants showed opposite effects across GI outcomes.
  - **TYMS rs11280056:** The deletion genotype was associated with lower risk of gastrointestinal disorders.
    - DEL/DEL vs INS/INS: OR = 0.100, p = 0.045.
  - **ERCC2 rs13181 (c.2251A>C):** C/C genotype was associated with lower risk of gastrointestinal disorders.
    - C/C vs A/A: OR = 0.100, p = 0.045.
  - **GSTP1 rs1695:** Despite its protective effect for neuropathy, the G allele was associated with increased mucositis risk.
    - Mucositis: OR = 2.27, p = 0.036.

- **Skin toxicity:** A TYMS promoter-region repeat variant was linked to higher skin toxicity risk.
  - **TYMS rs45445694 (5'UTR TSER):** The 3R/3R genotype was associated with increased risk of skin and subcutaneous tissue disorders.
    - Recessive model (3R/3R vs 2R/2R + 2R/3R): OR = 6.40, p = 0.029.

- **Pain-related adverse events:** Variants in TYMP, ABCB1, MTHFR, and ERCC2 were associated with pain risk.
  - **TYMP rs11479:** T-allele carriers had higher pain risk.
    - Dominant model (C/T + T/T vs C/C): OR = 5.860, p = 0.027.
  - **ABCB1 rs1128503:** C/C genotype was associated with increased pain risk.
    - Recessive model: OR = 4.200, p = 0.049.
  - **MTHFR rs1801131:** The C allele was associated with lower pain risk.
    - A/C vs A/A: OR = 0.355, p = 0.042.
    - Dominant model (A/C + C/C vs A/A): OR = 0.375, p = 0.048.
  - **ERCC2 rs13181:** In the multivariable pain model, C/C genotype increased pain risk.
    - OR = 6.272, p = 0.034.
  - **MTHFR rs1801131:** In the same multivariable model, A/C + C/C remained protective for pain.
    - OR = 0.159, p = 0.034.
    - Overall pain model: p = 0.01, accuracy 72.2%, AUC about 0.75.

- **Multivariable prediction models:** Two combined genotype models reached statistical significance.
  - **Anemia model:** Included **TYMP rs11479** and **ABCB1 rs1045642**.
    - TYMP T/T was associated with increased anemia risk: OR = 23.760, p = 0.038.
    - ABCB1 rs1045642 T/T showed a possible protective effect but was not statistically significant: OR = 0.153, p = 0.093.
    - Overall model: p = 0.027, accuracy 71.8%, AUC about 0.71.
  - **Pain model:** Included **ERCC2 rs13181** and **MTHFR rs1801131**.
    - ERCC2 C/C increased pain risk: OR = 6.272, p = 0.034.
    - MTHFR A/C + C/C decreased pain risk: OR = 0.159, p = 0.034.
    - Overall model: p = 0.01, accuracy 72.2%.

- **Variants without informative or significant associations in this cohort:** Not all tested markers contributed useful signals.
  - **DPYD rs3918290 and rs55886062:** No variability was observed in this sample, so they were excluded from association analyses.
  - **Other tested variants** such as **DPYD rs67376798, MTHFR rs1801133, ABCC2 rs717620, ABCC4 rs9561778, and ABCG2 rs2231142** showed no significant association with grouped or ungrouped toxicity outcomes in this study.
