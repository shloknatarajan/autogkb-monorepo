## Summary

## Background
This real-world pharmacogenetic study examined whether common variants in **SLCO1B1** (rs4149056, c.521T>C), **ABCG2** (rs2231142, c.421C>A), and **CYP2C9** (*2/*3; rs1799853 and rs1057910) were associated with **statin intolerance** in **2042 Finnish patients** starting statin therapy. Intolerance was defined mainly as **switching from the initial statin to another statin**, and secondarily as statin switching plus a **creatine kinase (CK) measurement** within a defined time window. Associations were tested separately for simvastatin, atorvastatin, rosuvastatin, fluvastatin, and pravastatin using Cox regression adjusted for statin dose.

## Key Findings
The clearest finding was for **SLCO1B1 rs4149056** and **simvastatin**. Patients with the **c.521C/C genotype** had a significantly higher risk of simvastatin intolerance than those with **c.521T/T**, whether intolerance was defined as **statin switching alone** (**HR 1.88, 95% CI 1.08–3.25, P=0.025**) or as **switching plus CK measurement** (**HR 5.44, 95% CI 1.49–19.9, P=0.011**). The **c.521T/C genotype** was not significantly associated with simvastatin intolerance. Higher simvastatin dose (40–80 mg vs 10 mg) was also associated with more switching (**HR 1.48, 95% CI 1.03–2.12, P=0.035**).

For **pravastatin**, carriers of the **SLCO1B1 c.521C allele** (**T/C or C/C**) had a higher risk of switching from pravastatin than **T/T** patients (**HR 2.11, 95% CI 1.01–4.39, P=0.047**). In contrast, **no significant associations** were found between **SLCO1B1** genotype and intolerance to **atorvastatin**, **rosuvastatin**, or **fluvastatin** in this cohort.

The study also found **no significant associations** between **ABCG2 rs2231142** and intolerance to **atorvastatin, rosuvastatin, or fluvastatin**, and **no significant association** between **CYP2C9** metabolizer status and **fluvastatin intolerance**. Overall, the main pharmacogenomic signal in this dataset was that **SLCO1B1 rs4149056** was associated with greater intolerance risk for **simvastatin**, and to a lesser extent **pravastatin**.
