## Summary

## Background
This prospective, multicenter nonrandomized clinical trial studied 2,910 South Korean patients with acute ischemic stroke who were treated with clopidogrel within 72 hours of stroke onset. Patients were genotyped for *CYP2C19* alleles (*1, *2, *3, *17) and grouped by metabolizer status. The main pharmacogenomic comparison was between **loss-of-function (LOF) allele carriers**—patients with *CYP2C19* *2 or *3 consistent with intermediate or poor metabolizer status—and **noncarriers** (extensive metabolizers: *1/*1, *1/*17, *17/*17).

## Key Findings
The key finding was that ***CYP2C19* LOF allele carriers had worse outcomes on clopidogrel** than noncarriers. Over 6 months, the primary composite cardiovascular outcome (recurrent ischemic or hemorrhagic stroke, myocardial infarction, or cardiovascular death) occurred in **2.7% of LOF carriers vs 1.6% of noncarriers** (**log-rank P = .048**). In multivariable Cox analysis, **noncarrier status was associated with lower risk** compared with LOF carrier status (**adjusted HR 0.58, 95% CI 0.33-0.99; P = .049**). Recurrent stroke was also more frequent in LOF carriers (**2.5% vs 1.4%; P = .047**).

Genotyping included *CYP2C19* *2, *3, *1, and *17, but the results were analyzed mainly by **carrier status of LOF alleles**, rather than by each allele separately. Among the 2,910 patients, **61.3% were LOF carriers**, including **47.8% intermediate metabolizers** and **13.5% poor metabolizers**. There was **no significant difference between intermediate and poor metabolizers** for the primary outcome (**HR 0.96, 95% CI 0.49-1.88; P = .92**).

Importantly, the genotype effect appeared to influence **clopidogrel effectiveness rather than safety**. Major bleeding was similar between groups (**0.6% in LOF carriers vs 0.8% in noncarriers; P = .56**), and all-cause mortality also did not differ significantly (**0.3% vs 0.1%; P = .27**). Overall, the study shows that *CYP2C19* LOF genotype is associated with reduced clinical benefit from clopidogrel after ischemic stroke.
