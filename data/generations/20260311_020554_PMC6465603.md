## Summary

## Background
This retrospective study evaluated 149 Chinese patients with autoimmune hepatitis (AIH) or AIH–primary biliary cholangitis variant syndrome who received azathioprine (AZA) for at least 3 months. The investigators genotyped **NUDT15 rs116855232 (R139C)** and **TPMT*3C rs1142345** by PCR/Sanger sequencing and examined their association with **AZA-induced leukopenia** (white blood cell count <3 × 10^9/L), AZA dose requirements, and thiopurine metabolite levels.

## Key Findings
The main pharmacogenomic signal was for **NUDT15 rs116855232**. AZA-induced leukopenia occurred in **12/149 patients (8.1%)** and was strongly associated with the **T risk allele**: **OR 20.41, 95% CI 7.84–53.13, P < 0.00001**. Under a dominant model, **CT/TT genotypes** had much higher leukopenia risk than **CC** (**OR 89.47, 95% CI 10.78–742.53, P < 0.0001**). Clinically, leukopenia developed in **9/24 (37.5%)** patients with **CT**, **2/2 (100%)** with **TT**, and only **1/123 (0.8%)** with **CC**. All leukopenia cases occurred within the first 60 days after starting AZA or increasing the dose. The T allele had **91.67% sensitivity** and **89.05% specificity** for predicting leukopenia in this cohort.

NUDT15 genotype also affected AZA handling and dose tolerance. Patients with **NUDT15 CT** had significantly higher **6-thioguanine nucleotide (6-TGN)** levels than **CC** patients (**276.6 vs 145.3 pmol/8 × 10^8 RBC, P = 0.002**) and required a lower AZA maintenance dose after remission (**0.96 vs 1.23 mg/kg/day, P = 0.028**). The dose-to-6-TGN ratio was also lower in **CT** carriers (**0.005 vs 0.007, P = 0.001**), consistent with greater thiopurine sensitivity. In contrast, **TPMT*3C rs1142345** was **not significantly associated** with leukopenia (**P = 0.283**). NUDT15 genotype was also **not associated** with biochemical remission rate (**P = 0.361**) or time to remission (**P = 0.161**).
