## Summary

## Background
This study evaluated whether **CYP2D6 genetic variation** affects outcomes in postmenopausal women with **estrogen receptor–positive early breast cancer** treated in ABCSG 8. Patients received either **tamoxifen for 5 years** (Arm A) or **tamoxifen for 2 years followed by anastrozole for 3 years** (Arm B). The investigators genotyped common CYP2D6 alleles linked to **no function** (*3, *4, *6), **reduced function** (*10, *41), or **extensive/normal metabolism** (absence of these alleles). The *5 deletion and gene duplications could not be assessed.

## Key Findings
The main pharmacogenomic signal was seen during **tamoxifen treatment only**. In women who stayed on **tamoxifen for 5 years** (Arm A), those with **two no-function alleles** (poor metabolizer phenotype; PM/PM, based on combinations of *3, *4, and/or *6) had a **significantly higher odds of a disease event** than women with **two normal-function alleles** (EM/EM): **OR 2.45, 95% CI 1.05–5.73, p=0.04**. Women carrying **one no-function allele** (PM/IM or PM/EM) also showed a borderline increase in risk: **OR 1.67, 95% CI 0.95–2.93, p=0.07**. By contrast, patients with **reduced-function alleles only** (*10 and/or *41; EM/IM or IM/IM) did **not** show a significant association with events on tamoxifen: **OR 1.23, 95% CI 0.58–2.61, p=0.60**.

When treatment periods were separated, the association remained limited to the **tamoxifen-exposed period**. During **years 3–5**, women still receiving tamoxifen in Arm A with the **PM/PM phenotype** had a trend toward more events versus EM/EM (**OR 2.40, 95% CI 0.86–6.66, p=0.09**), and those with **one no-function allele** also showed a similar trend (**OR 1.70, 95% CI 0.91–3.17, p=0.09**). In contrast, in Arm B, where patients had switched to **anastrozole**, **CYP2D6 phenotype was not associated with worse outcome** over 5 years (**PM/PM vs EM/EM: OR 0.60, 95% CI 0.15–2.37, p=0.47**; **PM/IM or PM/EM: OR 0.76, 95% CI 0.43–1.31, p=0.32**), and no significant association was seen during years 3–5 after the switch. Among individual variants, **CYP2D6*4** was the most common no-function allele and was in Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium; results were reported primarily by **metabolizer group**, not by single allele alone.
