## Summary

## Background
This retrospective study evaluated whether pretreatment **UGT1A1** genotyping could guide **irinotecan** dosing in patients with **advanced gastric cancer** receiving second-line therapy. Irinotecan dose was adjusted by **UGT1A1*6** genotype: **GG 125 mg/m²**, **GA 100 mg/m²**, and **AA 75 mg/m²**; some patients with **AA** were switched to **paclitaxel**. The study assessed toxicity, progression-free survival (PFS), and overall survival (OS), and also examined **UGT1A1*28**.

## Key Findings
- **Toxicity risk with genotype-guided irinotecan:** Toxicity was strongly associated with **UGT1A1*6**, but not with **UGT1A1*28**.
  - **UGT1A1*6:** Significant differences in irinotecan-related **delayed diarrhea** (*p* = 0.000), **neutropenia** (*p* = 0.000), and **leukopenia** (*p* = 0.003) were seen across **GG, GA, and AA** groups.
  - **UGT1A1*6 AA:** Patients with the **AA** genotype had the worst toxicity despite dose reduction. In Table 3, **grade 3/4 diarrhea, neutropenia, and leukopenia occurred in 100% (5/5)** of evaluable AA patients, versus **2%, 24%, and 19%** in **GG**, and **23%, 31%, and 31%** in **GA**, respectively.
  - **UGT1A1*28:** No significant association was found with irinotecan-related **diarrhea** (*p* = 0.136), **neutropenia** (*p* = 0.991), or **leukopenia** (*p* = 0.857).

- **Survival outcomes:** After genotype-guided dose adjustment, **UGT1A1*6** and **UGT1A1*28** were not associated with PFS or OS.
  - **UGT1A1*6:** Median **PFS** was **4.8 months (GG)**, **4.9 months (GA)**, and **4.4 months (AA)**; median **OS** was **9.3 months (GG)**, **9.3 months (GA)**, and **not reached (AA)**, with no significant differences by genotype.
  - **UGT1A1*6:** In regression analyses, **GA/AA vs GG** was not associated with **PFS** (univariable HR 1.290, 95% CI 0.811-2.052, *p* = 0.283; multivariable HR 1.325, 95% CI 0.792-2.219, *p* = 0.284) or **OS** (univariable HR 1.016, 95% CI 0.610-1.692, *p* = 0.951; multivariable HR 0.894, 95% CI 0.504-1.588, *p* = 0.703).
  - **UGT1A1*28:** Variant carriers (**TA6/TA7 + TA7/TA7 vs TA6/TA6**) were not associated with **PFS** (univariable HR 0.855, 95% CI 0.525-1.392, *p* = 0.528; multivariable HR 0.962, 95% CI 0.565-1.637, *p* = 0.887) or **OS** (univariable HR 1.108, 95% CI 0.648-1.893, *p* = 0.708; multivariable HR 0.992, 95% CI 0.529-1.861, *p* = 0.981).

- **Clinical biomarker context:** In this gastric cancer cohort, **UGT1A1*6** was the more clinically informative pharmacogenomic marker for irinotecan safety.
  - **UGT1A1*6 genotype frequencies:** **GG 60.9%**, **GA 31.8%**, **AA 7.3%**.
  - **UGT1A1*28 genotype frequencies:** **TA6/TA6 70.9%**, **TA6/TA7 25.5%**, **TA7/TA7 3.6%**.
  - **Practical implication from study results:** Patients with **UGT1A1*6 GG/GA** had similar survival after dose-adjusted irinotecan, while **UGT1A1*6 AA** patients had substantial severe toxicity and were often switched to **paclitaxel**.
