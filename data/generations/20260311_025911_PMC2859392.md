# Effects of CYP2B6 G516T polymorphisms on plasma efavirenz and nevirapine levels when co-administered with rifampicin in HIV/TB co-infected Thai adults

**PMCID:** PMC2859392  
**PMID:** 20338069  
**Generated:** 2026-03-11T02:59:11.426528

## Summary

## Background
This study evaluated whether genetic variation in drug-metabolizing enzymes affects efavirenz or nevirapine exposure in 124 Thai adults with HIV/TB coinfection who were receiving rifampicin-containing TB treatment. Patients received efavirenz-based ART (n=65) or nevirapine-based ART (n=59). The investigators genotyped 7 SNPs in **CYP2B6** and **CYP3A4**, then measured 12-hour post-dose plasma drug concentrations at weeks 6 and 12 of ART and again 1 month after rifampicin was stopped.

## Key Findings
The main pharmacogenomic signal was **CYP2B6 G516T (rs3745274)**. In the efavirenz group, patients with the **TT genotype** had much higher plasma efavirenz concentrations than those with **GT** or **GG** at all time points. Mean efavirenz concentrations in TT carriers were **10.97, 13.62, and 8.48 mg/L** at week 6, week 12, and 1 month after rifampicin discontinuation, versus **3.43, 3.35, and 3.21 mg/L** for GT and **2.88, 2.45, and 2.08 mg/L** for GG; all comparisons were **p < 0.0001**. This indicates a strong association between **CYP2B6 516TT** and higher efavirenz exposure despite rifampicin co-treatment.

For nevirapine, **CYP2B6 516TT** was also associated with higher drug levels, but the evidence was weaker, likely because very few patients had TT genotype in that arm. Mean nevirapine concentrations in TT carriers were **14.09, 7.94, and 9.44 mg/L** at week 6, week 12, and after rifampicin discontinuation, compared with **5.65, 5.58, and 7.03 mg/L** for GT and **5.42, 5.34, and 6.43 mg/L** for GG. This difference reached significance at **week 6 (p = 0.003)** but not at **week 12 (p = 0.409)** or after rifampicin discontinuation (**p = 0.448**). There was also only a **nonsignificant trend** toward better week-12 viral suppression (<50 copies/mL) in TT carriers for both efavirenz (**88.9% vs 77.4% GT vs 68.0% GG; p = 0.430**) and nevirapine (**100% vs 71.0% GT vs 60.0% GG; p = 0.288**). Other tested variants in **CYP2B6** and **CYP3A4** were either not variable enough for analysis or did not show a clear association with drug levels.
