## Summary

## Background
This in vitro pharmacogenomics study tested how 22 CYP3A4 variants found in the Chinese Han population affect metabolism of ticagrelor, an antiplatelet drug mainly cleared by CYP3A4. Recombinant CYP3A4 proteins expressed in insect cells were incubated with ticagrelor, and formation of the active metabolite AR-C124910XX was measured by UHPLC-MS/MS. Enzyme activity was summarized using Michaelis-Menten kinetics, especially intrinsic clearance (CLint = Vmax/Km), and compared with wild-type CYP3A4*1.

## Key Findings
- **Variants with increased ticagrelor metabolism:** Three alleles showed significantly higher intrinsic clearance than wild-type, suggesting faster conversion of ticagrelor to AR-C124910XX in this assay.
  - **CYP3A4*11:** CLint 2,123.33 µL/min/nmol, or **264.73% of wild-type** (**p < 0.01** vs *1); the largest increase observed.
  - **CYP3A4*18:** CLint 977.79 µL/min/nmol, or **122.49% of wild-type** (**p < 0.01**).
  - **CYP3A4*33:** CLint 940.29 µL/min/nmol, or **117.77% of wild-type** (**p < 0.01**).

- **Variants with no significant change in ticagrelor metabolism:** Four alleles had intrinsic clearance similar to wild-type CYP3A4*1.
  - **CYP3A4*10:** CLint 889.66 µL/min/nmol, **111.42% of wild-type**; not significantly different.
  - **CYP3A4*23:** CLint 742.86 µL/min/nmol, **92.82% of wild-type**; not significantly different.
  - **CYP3A4*32:** CLint 833.05 µL/min/nmol, **103.77% of wild-type**; not significantly different.
  - **CYP3A4*34:** CLint 893.15 µL/min/nmol, **111.93% of wild-type**; not significantly different.

- **Variants with reduced ticagrelor metabolism:** Fourteen alleles had significantly lower intrinsic clearance than wild-type (**p < 0.01** for all listed below), indicating reduced metabolic activity toward ticagrelor.
  - **Markedly reduced activity:**
    - **CYP3A4*24:** CLint 11.92 µL/min/nmol, **1.48% of wild-type**.
    - **CYP3A4*17:** CLint 37.75 µL/min/nmol, **4.72% of wild-type**.
  - **Moderately reduced activity:**
    - **CYP3A4*2:** 203.59 µL/min/nmol, **25.52% of wild-type**.
    - **CYP3A4*14:** 229.84 µL/min/nmol, **28.74%**.
    - **CYP3A4*3:** 280.23 µL/min/nmol, **35.01%**.
    - **CYP3A4*16:** 360.07 µL/min/nmol, **45.07%**.
    - **CYP3A4*5:** 369.89 µL/min/nmol, **46.25%**.
    - **CYP3A4*28:** 425.14 µL/min/nmol, **53.32%**.
    - **CYP3A4*29:** 531.30 µL/min/nmol, **66.46%**.
    - **CYP3A4*19:** 539.74 µL/min/nmol, **67.37%**.
    - **CYP3A4*4:** 551.89 µL/min/nmol, **68.84%**.
    - **CYP3A4*15:** 573.55 µL/min/nmol, **71.80%**.
    - **CYP3A4*9:** 599.66 µL/min/nmol, **74.97%**.
    - **CYP3A4*31:** 601.16 µL/min/nmol, **75.11%**.

- **Loss-of-function variant:** One allele had no measurable ticagrelor-metabolizing activity in this system.
  - **CYP3A4*30:** No detectable AR-C124910XX formation; kinetic parameters were **not determined**.

- **Reference enzyme activity and kinetic context:** Wild-type CYP3A4*1 served as the comparison standard.
  - **CYP3A4*1:** Vmax **1,743.33 pmol/min/nmol**, Km **2.18 µM**, CLint **800.45 µL/min/nmol**.
  - **Overall pattern:** Nearly all variants showed altered Vmax and/or Km, and all measured variants had higher Km than wild-type, with differences in CLint driving the classification of increased, unchanged, or decreased ticagrelor metabolism.
