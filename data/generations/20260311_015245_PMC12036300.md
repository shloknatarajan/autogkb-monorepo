# The frequencies of CYP2C19*2, *3, and *17 alleles and their impact on the clinical efficacy of doubled maintenance dose of clopidogrel in Syrian patients with coronary artery disease

## Summary

## Background
This prospective cohort study evaluated whether common **CYP2C19** variants affect response to **clopidogrel** in **Syrian patients with coronary artery disease undergoing PCI**. Fifty patients received dual antiplatelet therapy with a **doubled clopidogrel maintenance dose (150 mg/day)**, and genotyping for **CYP2C19*2 (rs4244285), *3 (rs4986893), and *17 (rs12248560)** was performed by PCR followed by Sanger sequencing. Clinical outcomes were tracked for **18–24 months**, including recurrent angina, major adverse cardiovascular events (MACE), and bleeding.

## Key Findings
The main pharmacogenomic result was that **CYP2C19*2**, a loss-of-function allele, **was not significantly associated with worse clopidogrel outcomes** in this cohort receiving high-dose clopidogrel. Among patients with follow-up data, **refractory angina** occurred in **33.3% of *2 carriers vs 38.9% of non-carriers** (**P > 0.9999**), and **MACE** occurred in **16.7% of *2 carriers vs 2.9% of non-carriers** (**P = 0.2744**). **Bleeding events** were also not significantly different for *2 carriers (**33.3% vs 5.7%**, **P = 0.0952**), although numbers were small.

For **CYP2C19*17**, a gain-of-function allele, **no significant association with bleeding** was found despite a numerically higher bleeding rate in carriers: **16.7% in *17 carriers vs 6.9% in non-carriers** (**P = 0.567**). In this Syrian cohort, allele frequencies were **8% for *2**, **0% for *3**, and **17% for *17**. Phenotype distribution was mostly **normal metabolizers (56%)**, followed by **intermediate metabolizers (12%)**, **rapid metabolizers (26%)**, and **ultrarapid metabolizers (4%)**; **poor metabolizers were rare (2%)**. No *3, *10, *22, *26, or *33 alleles were detected. Overall, in this small study of patients treated with **doubled-dose clopidogrel**, the tested **CYP2C19 variants were not significantly associated with ischemic or bleeding outcomes**.
