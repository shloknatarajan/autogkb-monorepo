## Summary

## Background
This pediatric case-control study examined whether **HLA-A*31:01** and **HLA-B*15:02** predict **carbamazepine (CBZ) hypersensitivity** in Canadian children with seizure disorders. The study included **42 children with CBZ hypersensitivity** and **91 CBZ-tolerant controls**. Hypersensitivity phenotypes included **Stevens-Johnson syndrome/toxic epidermal necrolysis (SJS/TEN)**, **hypersensitivity syndrome (HSS)**, and **maculopapular exanthema (MPE)**. Genotyping used direct testing for **HLA-B*15:02**, direct sequence-based typing for **HLA-A*31:01**, and the proxy SNP **rs1061235**.

## Key Findings
- **Risk of CBZ hypersensitivity syndrome and milder rash:** **HLA-A*31:01** was strongly linked to CBZ-induced **HSS** and **MPE**, but not to SJS/TEN.
  - **HLA-A*31:01:** Associated with **CBZ-HSS**: 3/6 cases vs 3/91 controls; **OR 26.36**, **p=0.0025**.
  - **HLA-A*31:01:** Associated with **CBZ-MPE**: 6/26 cases vs 3/91 controls; **OR 8.57**, **p=0.0037**.
  - **HLA-A*31:01:** For **HSS/MPE combined**, 9/32 cases carried the allele; **OR 11.18**, **p=2.6×10⁻4**.
  - **HLA-A*31:01:** For **all CBZ hypersensitivity cases combined**, association remained significant; **OR 7.85**, **p=0.0016**.
  - **HLA-A*31:01:** **Not associated with CBZ-SJS/TEN**; no carriers were found among 9 SJS/TEN cases; **OR 1.33**, **p=1.000**.

- **Risk of severe cutaneous reaction (SJS/TEN):** **HLA-B*15:02** was specifically associated with **CBZ-SJS/TEN**.
  - **HLA-B*15:02:** Associated with **CBZ-SJS/TEN**: 3/9 cases carried the allele; **OR 38.65**, **p=0.0022**.
  - **HLA-B*15:02:** **Not associated with CBZ-HSS**; **p=1.000**.
  - **HLA-B*15:02:** **Not associated with CBZ-MPE**; **p=1.000**.
  - **HLA-B*15:02:** **Not significantly associated with all CBZ hypersensitivity cases combined**; **OR 6.51**, **p=0.101**.

- **Proxy marker performance:** The SNP **rs1061235** tracked with hypersensitivity risk, especially for HSS/MPE, but was an imperfect surrogate for **HLA-A*31:01** in this ethnically diverse cohort.
  - **rs1061235 T allele:** Associated with **CBZ-HSS**; **OR 25.97**, **p=8.2×10⁻4**.
  - **rs1061235 T allele:** Associated with **CBZ-MPE**; **OR 7.32**, **p=7.4×10⁻4**.
  - **rs1061235 T allele:** Associated with **HSS/MPE combined**; **OR 9.45**, **p=2.6×10⁻5**.
  - **rs1061235 T allele:** Associated with **all CBZ hypersensitivity cases combined**; **OR 6.96**, **p=1.5×10⁻4**.
  - **rs1061235 T allele:** **Not associated with CBZ-SJS/TEN**; **OR 0.69**, **p=1.000**.
  - **Proxy marker context:** Of 20 children carrying **rs1061235T**, only 12 also had **HLA-A*31:01** on direct typing; some non-HLA-A*31:01 carriers instead had **HLA-A*33:03** or **HLA-A*33:01**, showing rs1061235 is not a reliable stand-alone proxy across ancestries.

- **Combined biomarker signal:** Considering either major risk allele improved detection of children at increased risk for CBZ hypersensitivity.
  - **HLA-A*31:01 or HLA-B*15:02:** 12/42 CBZ hypersensitivity cases vs 4/87 controls carried at least one risk allele; **OR 8.14**, **p=2.6×10⁻4**.
  - **Phenotype-specific pattern:** In this study, **HLA-A*31:01** marked risk for **HSS/MPE**, while **HLA-B*15:02** marked risk for **SJS/TEN**.
