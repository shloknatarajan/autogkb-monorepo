## Summary

## Background
This retrospective cohort study examined whether **CYP2D6 genetic variation** affects the risk of **adverse events (AEs) from risperidone** in children and adolescents. The authors identified 257 patients aged 18 years or younger who had taken risperidone for at least 4 weeks, then used **CYP2D6 sequencing plus copy-number testing** to assign metabolizer phenotypes: poor, intermediate, normal, or ultrarapid.

## Key Findings
- **CYP2D6 metabolizer status and risperidone safety:** Reduced CYP2D6 function was associated with a higher risk of risperidone-related adverse events.
  - **CYP2D6 poor/intermediate metabolizers vs normal/ultrarapid metabolizers:** Adverse events occurred more often in poor/intermediate metabolizers than in normal/ultrarapid metabolizers (**46% [15/33] vs 27% [61/224]; P=0.04**).
  - **CYP2D6 poor/intermediate metabolizers:** After adjustment for age, sex, race, and initial risperidone dose, poor/intermediate metabolizers had a significantly higher odds of adverse events (**adjusted OR 2.4, 95% CI 1.1–5.1; P=0.03**).

- **Phenotype distribution in the cohort:** The study evaluated CYP2D6 at the phenotype level rather than testing individual variants for separate associations.
  - **CYP2D6 phenotypes identified:** **15 poor metabolizers (6%)**, **18 intermediate metabolizers (7%)**, **218 normal metabolizers (85%)**, and **6 ultrarapid metabolizers (2%)**.
  - **Individual star alleles/variants:** Although multiple **CYP2D6 star alleles and copy-number changes** were used to determine phenotype, the paper **did not report variant-specific associations** for individual alleles such as *4, *5, *10, or duplications. The main reported pharmacogenomic result was the association between **overall CYP2D6 metabolizer group** and AE risk.

- **Drug–gene vs drug–drug interaction context:** Concomitant strong CYP2D6 inhibitors did not explain AE risk among patients with normal/ultrarapid CYP2D6 status.
  - **CYP2D6 inhibitor use in normal/ultrarapid metabolizers:** AE frequency was similar with or without a strong CYP2D6 inhibitor (**30% [6/20] vs 27% [55/204]; P=0.8**).
  - **Adjusted analysis:** Strong CYP2D6 inhibitor use was **not significantly associated** with AEs (**adjusted OR 1.2, 95% CI 0.4–3.3; P=0.7**).
