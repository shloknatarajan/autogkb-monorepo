# Shared peptide binding of HLA Class I and II alleles associate with cutaneous nevirapine hypersensitivity and identify novel risk alleles

**PMCID:** PMC5561238

## Summary

## Background
This case-control pharmacogenomic study examined severe cutaneous nevirapine hypersensitivity reactions (grade 3/4 rash) in 151 HIV-1-infected cases and 413 nevirapine-tolerant controls. High-resolution HLA typing was performed, and the analysis focused on whether shared HLA peptide-binding pocket features, rather than single alleles alone, explain risk across different ancestry groups.

## Key Findings
The strongest and most consistent risk signal was **HLA-C*04:01**, which was associated with severe cutaneous nevirapine hypersensitivity across ancestries: overall **OR 3.06, p=0.0001**, with similar effects in Asians (**OR 5.49, p=0.0001**), Caucasians (**OR 2.08, p=0.02**), and Africans (**OR 3.84, p=0.04**). In multivariable analysis, **HLA-C*04:01** remained the primary risk allele (**OR 4.06, 95% CI 2.39–6.88**). Additional risk alleles shared a similar **HLA-C F-pocket** structure, including **HLA-C*05:01** (Caucasians: **OR 2.84, p=0.002**) and **HLA-C*18:01** (suggestive in Africans), and the broader “other risk F-pocket” cluster (**HLA-C*04:03/*04:06/*04:07, *05:01/*05:09, *18:01**) was also associated with risk (**OR 2.91, 95% CI 1.62–5.23**).

A protective association was seen for a group of **HLA-B alleles with a shared B-pocket motif**, especially the **HLA-B*15** group. At the 2-digit level, **HLA-B*15** was protective (**OR 0.40, 95% CI 0.23–0.70, p=0.001** after adjustment for ethnicity and the HLA-C risk cluster). In the multivariable model, the protective cluster (**HLA-B*15:01/*12/*24/*25/*27/*32/*35 and HLA-B*52:01**) showed a strong reduction in risk (**OR 0.18, 95% CI 0.07–0.46**). By contrast, some secondary **HLA-B** risk groups remained after adjustment for the primary HLA-C effect, including **HLA-B*51/*54/*55/*56** (**OR 2.09, 95% CI 1.29–3.39, p=0.003**) and **HLA-B*38/*39** (**OR 1.82, 95% CI 1.02–3.22, p=0.04**). **HLA-B*35:05** was the main B*35 subtype linked to risk; the **HLA-B*35** group as a whole was not associated.

A secondary class II signal was identified for an **HLA-DRB1 P4-pocket risk cluster**, including **HLA-DRB1*01:01/*01:02/*01:03 and *04:04/*04:05/*04:08/*04:10**, with overall **OR 2.00, 95% CI 1.23–3.24**. This effect was stronger in Asians (**OR 3.23, 95% CI 1.40–7.43**). In contrast, **HLA-DRB1*04:01** and **HLA-DRB1*04:15** appeared protective in Caucasians (**2 cases vs 26 controls, p=0.01**). The multivariable model also showed that **slow CYP2B6 metabolizer genotype** independently increased risk (**OR 2.07, 95% CI 1.12–3.82**), indicating that both immune-related HLA variation and drug metabolism contribute to severe cutaneous nevirapine hypersensitivity.
