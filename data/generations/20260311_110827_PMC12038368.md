## Summary

## Background
This study evaluated whether common pharmacokinetic gene variants affect steady-state plasma concentrations of simvastatin in 89 Thai patients with dyslipidemia or coronary artery disease. Patients had been taking simvastatin for at least 2 weeks without dose changes. The investigators measured 12-hour post-dose plasma simvastatin lactone (SVL) and simvastatin acid (SVA, the active form) and genotyped 10 variants in transporter and metabolizing enzyme genes.

## Key Findings
- **Higher simvastatin acid exposure linked to reduced SLCO1B1 function:** The clearest pharmacogenomic signal was in *SLCO1B1*, which encodes the hepatic uptake transporter OATP1B1.
  - **SLCO1B1 rs4149056 (c.521T>C):** Carriers of TC+CC had significantly higher dose-normalized simvastatin acid concentrations than TT carriers (median 0.53 vs 0.19 ng/mL, *p* = 0.03).
  - **SLCO1B1 diplotype *1b/*15:** Patients with *1b/*15 had significantly higher simvastatin acid concentrations than those with *1a/*1a* (0.58 vs 0.16 ng/mL, *p* < 0.001).
  - **OATP1B1 decreased-function group:** Patients classified as decreased function (*1b/*5 or *1b/*15) showed higher simvastatin acid than normal-function carriers, but this was not statistically significant overall (*p* = 0.07).

- **Dose-specific SLCO1B1 effects at 10 mg/day:** In subgroup analysis, *SLCO1B1* effects were most evident at the 10 mg/day dose.
  - **SLCO1B1 rs4149056 (c.521T>C):** TC+CC carriers had higher simvastatin acid than TT carriers at 10 mg/day (5.83 vs 1.95 ng/mL, *p* = 0.06), suggesting a trend but not formal significance.
  - **SLCO1B1 rs2306283 (c.388A>G):** AG+GG carriers had significantly higher simvastatin acid than AA carriers at 10 mg/day (3.63 vs 1.59 ng/mL, *p* = 0.04).
  - **SLCO1B1 rs2306283 overall:** No significant association with simvastatin acid in the full cohort (0.23 vs 0.16 ng/mL, *p* = 0.13).

- **No significant genetic effects on simvastatin lactone concentrations:** None of the tested variants significantly affected dose-normalized SVL levels.
  - **SLCO1B1 rs4149056:** No association with SVL (*p* = 0.33).
  - **SLCO1B1 *1b/*15:** No association with SVL compared with *1a/*1a* (*p* = 0.71).

- **No meaningful associations for other transporter and CYP variants:** The other candidate pharmacokinetic variants were not significantly associated with steady-state simvastatin acid or lactone concentrations in this cohort.
  - **ABCB1 rs1045642:** No association with SVA (*p* = 1.00) or SVL (*p* = 0.70).
  - **ABCC2 rs717620:** No association with SVA (*p* = 0.67) or SVL (*p* = 0.45).
  - **ABCC2 rs3740066:** No association with SVA (*p* = 0.84) or SVL (*p* = 0.96).
  - **ABCG2 rs2231142:** No association with SVA (*p* = 0.11) or SVL (*p* = 0.54).
  - **SLCO1B3 rs7311158 / rs4149117:** No association with SVA (*p* = 0.86 / 0.94) or SVL (*p* = 0.46 / 0.21).
  - **CYP3A4*1G rs2242480:** No association with SVA (*p* = 0.16) or SVL (*p* = 0.92).
  - **CYP3A5*3 rs776746:** No association with SVA (*p* = 0.23) or SVL (*p* = 0.65).

- **Study population variant frequencies relevant to interpretation:** The major *SLCO1B1* variants were present at measurable frequencies in this Thai cohort.
  - **SLCO1B1 rs4149056:** TT 89.89%, TC 10.11%, CC 0%; C allele frequency 5.06%.
  - **SLCO1B1 rs2306283:** AA 19.10%, AG 37.08%, GG 43.82%; G allele frequency 62.36%.

Overall, this study supports *SLCO1B1*—especially rs4149056 and the *1b/*15 diplotype—as the main pharmacogenomic determinant of higher steady-state simvastatin acid exposure in Thai patients.
