# Effect of NLRP3 inflammasome genes polymorphism on disease susceptibility and response to TNF-α inhibitors in Iraqi patients with rheumatoid arthritis

## Summary

## Background
This Iraqi case-control study evaluated whether two inflammasome-related variants—**CARD8 rs2043211 (A>T)** and **NLRP3 rs4612666 (C>T)**—are associated with rheumatoid arthritis (RA) severity and response to **TNF-α inhibitors** (**etanercept** or **infliximab**). The study included **100 RA patients** and **100 healthy controls**. Genotyping was performed by SYBR Green real-time PCR, and treatment response after 3 months was assessed using **DAS-28/EULAR-based criteria**.

## Key Findings
Neither variant was significantly associated with **RA susceptibility** in this cohort. Genotype and allele frequencies were similar between patients and controls for **CARD8 rs2043211** (genotypes **P=0.17**, alleles **P=0.059**) and **NLRP3 rs4612666** (genotypes **P=0.08**, alleles **P=0.879**).

The main pharmacogenomic signal was for **treatment response**. In RA patients receiving anti-TNF therapy, the **CARD8 rs2043211 TT genotype** was associated with **worse outcomes after treatment**: higher DAS-28 (**4.65±0.65** for TT vs **3.51±0.90** for AA, **P<0.0001**), higher **TNF-α** (**14.25±4.67** vs **5.64±3.56 pg/mL**, **P<0.0001**), higher **IL-1β** (**4.55±0.64** vs **1.05±0.34 pg/mL**, **P<0.0001**), and a much lower response rate (**1 responder / 15 non-responders** for TT vs **32 / 5** for AA; genotype distribution by response **P=0.0001**). Model-based analysis also showed better response in **AA+AT vs TT** (**P<0.0001**, OR **0.019**, 95% CI **0.002–0.157**) and poorer response in **AT+TT vs AA** (**P=0.001**, OR **0.183**, 95% CI **0.063–0.531**).

Similarly, the **NLRP3 rs4612666 TT genotype** was linked to **reduced anti-TNF response** and more active disease: DAS-28 **4.72±0.55** for TT vs **3.52±0.89** for CC (**P<0.0001**), **TNF-α** **14.74±4.61** vs **5.56±3.31 pg/mL** (**P<0.0001**), **IL-1β** **4.86±0.50** vs **1.18±0.39 pg/mL** (**P<0.0001**), and fewer responders (**1 / 10** for TT vs **41 / 6** for CC; genotype distribution by response **P=0.0001**). Patients with **CC+CT vs TT** had better response (**P<0.0001**, OR **0.037**, 95% CI **0.004–0.304**), while **CT+TT vs CC** was associated with poorer response (**P<0.0001**, OR **0.131**, 95% CI **0.047–0.36**). Overall, in this cohort, **T-allele-bearing genotypes in both CARD8 and NLRP3 were associated with lower response to etanercept/infliximab and higher residual inflammatory activity after treatment**.
