## Summary

## Background
This retrospective case-control study examined pharmacogenomic predictors of severe nevirapine toxicity in adults with HIV-1 infection who had baseline CD4 counts above 150 cells/μl. Cases had grade III/IV cutaneous reactions and/or symptomatic grade 3 or higher hepatic events within 8 weeks of starting nevirapine; controls tolerated nevirapine for more than 18 weeks without these events. The investigators analyzed 123 HLA alleles and 2744 SNPs in MHC and drug metabolism/transport genes across African, Asian, and European ancestry groups.

## Key Findings
Severe **cutaneous adverse events** were associated with both **drug metabolism** and **HLA** variants. The main non-HLA signal was **CYP2B6 516G>T (rs3745274)** and linked SNPs **rs2054675** and **rs3786547**. In the overall analysis, cutaneous events were associated with **CYP2B6 516G>T** (**OR 1.66**). Increasing numbers of the 516T allele were associated with higher rash risk in **Black patients (P=0.0025)** and **White patients (P=0.021)**, with a weaker trend in Asians (**P=0.12**); among Blacks, **516TT vs GG** gave an **OR 5.92 (95% CI 1.53–26.8)**. The linked CYP2B6 SNPs **rs2054675** and **rs3786547** remained significant for cutaneous events after multiple-testing correction, and in Asians these two markers showed stronger association than rs3745274 itself (**both P=0.041**). Among HLA markers, **HLA-Cw*04** was associated with cutaneous events in the full cohort (**OR 2.51**), and **HLA-B*35** was associated in **Asians (OR 3.47)**, especially **Thais (OR 5.65)**.

Risk was substantially higher when risk variants occurred together. Among **Black patients**, the combination of **CYP2B6 516TT + HLA-Cw*04** was associated with very high cutaneous risk (**OR 18.90**); in the total population, this combination had **OR 6.31 (95% CI 2.53–15.73)** versus **516GG without HLA-Cw*04**. Among **Asian patients**, carrying both **HLA-B*35 and HLA-Cw*04** markedly increased cutaneous risk (**OR 18.34, 95% CI 5.10–65.99**). For **hepatic adverse events**, the main association was **HLA-DRB1*01** in **White patients** (**OR 3.02**), which became stronger when cases with both rash and hepatitis were excluded (**OR 3.63**). **HLA-DQB1*05** also showed an association with hepatic events in Whites, but this did not remain when only isolated hepatic cases were considered. No SNP, including **CYP2B6** variants, was significantly associated with hepatic events after correction for multiple testing. The study also found **no association for HLA-Cw*08** with either cutaneous or hepatic toxicity.
