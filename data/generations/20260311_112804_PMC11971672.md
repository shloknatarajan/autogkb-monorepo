## Summary

## Background
This prospective, multicenter nonrandomized clinical trial studied 2,910 South Korean patients with acute ischemic stroke who were treated with clopidogrel within 72 hours of stroke onset. Patients were genotyped for **CYP2C19 *1, *2, *3, and *17** and grouped as:

- **LOF allele carriers** (intermediate or poor metabolizers): ***1/*2, *1/*3, *2/*17, *3/*17, *2/*2, *2/*3, *3/*3**
- **LOF allele noncarriers** (extensive metabolizers): ***1/*1, *1/*17, *17/*17**

The main question was whether **CYP2C19 loss-of-function (LOF) status** affected clinical outcomes during clopidogrel treatment after ischemic stroke.

## Key Findings
- **Clopidogrel effectiveness:** Patients carrying a **CYP2C19 LOF allele** had worse cardiovascular outcomes on clopidogrel than noncarriers.
  - **CYP2C19*2 / CYP2C19*3 (LOF alleles):** Carriers of genotypes containing *2 or *3 had a higher 6-month risk of the composite cardiovascular outcome (stroke, myocardial infarction, or cardiovascular death) than noncarriers: **2.7% vs 1.6%**; log-rank **P = .048**. In multivariable Cox analysis, LOF noncarriers had lower risk than carriers (**adjusted HR 0.58, 95% CI 0.33-0.99; P = .049**).
  - **CYP2C19*1 / CYP2C19*17 non-LOF genotypes:** Patients with ***1/*1, *1/*17, or *17/*17** had better outcomes on clopidogrel than LOF carriers, consistent with preserved clopidogrel metabolism.

- **Recurrent stroke risk:** The main outcome difference was driven largely by recurrent stroke.
  - **CYP2C19*2 / CYP2C19*3 (LOF alleles):** Recurrent stroke occurred more often in LOF carriers than noncarriers: **2.5% vs 1.4%**; **P = .047**.
  - **Ischemic stroke specifically:** Ischemic stroke was numerically higher in LOF carriers (**2.4% vs 1.4%**) but did not reach statistical significance (**P = .06**).

- **Bleeding and mortality safety outcomes:** LOF status did not significantly affect clopidogrel safety outcomes in this study.
  - **CYP2C19*2 / CYP2C19*3 (LOF alleles):** Major bleeding was similar between groups: **0.6% in carriers vs 0.8% in noncarriers**; **HR 1.29, 95% CI 0.53-3.13; P = .56**.
  - **CYP2C19*2 / CYP2C19*3 (LOF alleles):** All-cause mortality was also not significantly different: **0.3% vs 0.1%**; **P = .27**.

- **Metabolizer subgroup analysis:** The study did not find a clear difference between intermediate and poor metabolizers among LOF carriers.
  - **CYP2C19 intermediate vs poor metabolizer status:** Comparing intermediate metabolizers with poor metabolizers, there was **no significant difference** in the primary outcome (**HR 0.96, 95% CI 0.49-1.88; P = .92**).

- **Variant classification context:** The study included **CYP2C19*17** in genotype classification, but the main reported pharmacogenomic result was based on **LOF carrier vs noncarrier status**, not on a separate *17-specific effect.
  - **CYP2C19*17:** Considered part of the noncarrier/extensive metabolizer group when paired as ***1/*17 or *17/*17**, but no independent outcome analysis for *17 alone was reported.
