## Summary

## Background
This retrospective nested case-control study evaluated 82 Chilean patients with advanced colorectal cancer treated with 5-fluorouracil (5-FU)-based regimens, including FOLFOX, CAPOX, FOLFIRI, capecitabine, and 5-FU/leucovorin. The authors genotyped 16 variants in genes involved in fluoropyrimidine metabolism, folate metabolism, oxaliplatin detoxification, DNA repair, and drug transport, and tested their associations with chemotherapy-related adverse drug reactions using logistic regression.

## Key Findings
The clearest pharmacogenomic signals involved **GSTP1, DPYD, and TYMS**. The **GSTP1 rs1695 G allele** was associated with **lower risk of neuropathy** (reported OR 0.147, p=0.012; supplementary analysis OR 0.01, p=0.008) and lower risk of broader nervous system toxicity, and it also reduced hematologic toxicity in genotype-based models (dominant model OR 0.298, p=0.001; G/G OR 0.157, p=0.0005). However, the same variant was associated with **higher mucositis risk** (OR 2.27, p=0.036). The **DPYD rs1801265 C allele** was linked to **higher neuropathy risk** (dominant model OR 4.583, p=0.05; T/C genotype OR 30.0, p=0.0049). In contrast, the high-risk guideline **DPYD** variants **rs3918290 (*DPYD*2A)** and **rs55886062 (*DPYD*13)** showed no variability in this cohort, so they could not be analyzed.

For **TYMS**, the **3′UTR 6-bp deletion rs11280056** was consistently **protective against hematologic toxicity**: INS/DEL OR 0.156 (p=0.002), DEL/DEL OR 0.029 (p=0.001), dominant model OR 0.107 (p<0.0001), and it also reduced **severe hematologic toxicity** (OR 0.098, p=0.005). The same deletion was associated with lower gastrointestinal toxicity (DEL/DEL OR 0.100, p=0.045). By contrast, the **TYMS 5′UTR rs45445694 3R/3R genotype** was associated with **higher risk of skin/subcutaneous toxicity** (OR 6.40, p=0.029). Additional associations included **TYMP rs11479** with **higher pain risk** (C/T+T/T vs C/C: OR 5.86, p=0.027), **ABCB1 rs1128503 C/C** with **higher pain risk** (OR 4.20, p=0.049), **MTHFR rs1801131 C carriers** with **lower pain risk** (A/C OR 0.355, p=0.042; dominant model OR 0.375, p=0.048) but **higher nervous system toxicity** (OR 5.805, p=0.035), and **ERCC2 rs13181 C/C** with **lower gastrointestinal toxicity** (OR 0.100, p=0.045).

Two multivariable toxicity models were also developed. An **anemia model** including **TYMP rs11479** and **ABCB1 rs1045642** was significant overall (model p=0.027), with **TYMP T/T** associated with increased anemia risk (OR 23.76, p=0.038), while **ABCB1 rs1045642 T/T** was not individually significant (OR 0.153, p=0.093). A **pain model** including **MTHFR rs1801131** and **ERCC2 rs13181** was also significant (model p=0.01): **MTHFR A/C+C/C** was protective (OR 0.159, p=0.034) and **ERCC2 C/C** increased risk (OR 6.272, p=0.034). Other tested variants, including **DPYD rs67376798, ABCC2 rs717620, ABCC4 rs9561778, ABCG2 rs2231142, and MTHFR rs1801133**, were not significantly associated with toxicity in this study.
