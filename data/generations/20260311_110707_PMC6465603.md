## Summary

## Background
This retrospective study evaluated 149 Chinese patients with autoimmune hepatitis (AIH) or AIH–primary biliary cholangitis variant syndrome who received azathioprine (AZA) for at least 3 months. The researchers genotyped **NUDT15 rs116855232 (R139C)** and **TPMT*3C rs1142345** to assess whether these variants were associated with AZA-induced leukopenia and AZA dose tolerance.

## Key Findings
- **Risk of azathioprine-induced leukopenia:** NUDT15 was the main genetic predictor of leukopenia in this cohort.
  - **NUDT15 rs116855232:** The **T allele** was strongly associated with AZA-induced leukopenia versus the C allele (**OR 20.41, 95% CI 7.84–53.13; P < 0.00001**), with **91.67% sensitivity** and **89.05% specificity**.
  - **NUDT15 rs116855232:** Leukopenia occurred in **9/24 (37.5%)** patients with **CT**, **2/2 (100%)** with **TT**, and **1/123 (0.8%)** with **CC**.
  - **NUDT15 rs116855232:** Under a dominant model, **CT+TT vs CC** was strongly associated with leukopenia (**OR 89.47, 95% CI 10.78–742.53; P < 0.0001**).
  - **NUDT15 rs116855232:** Under a recessive model, **TT vs CC+CT** was also associated with leukopenia (**OR 65.48, 95% CI 2.95–1454.21; P = 0.008**).
  - **NUDT15 rs116855232:** All leukopenia cases occurred within the first **60 days** after AZA initiation or dose increase. One **TT** patient developed **grade 4, life-threatening myelosuppression** within 4 weeks.

- **Effect on AZA metabolite levels and tolerated dose:** Carriers of the NUDT15 variant showed greater thiopurine exposure and required lower maintenance doses.
  - **NUDT15 rs116855232:** Patients with **CT** had significantly higher **6-thioguanine nucleotide (6-TGN)** levels than **CC** patients: **276.6 vs 145.3 pmol/8×10^8 RBC** (**P = 0.002**).
  - **NUDT15 rs116855232:** Among patients in biochemical remission, the median AZA maintenance dose was lower in **CT** than **CC** carriers: **0.96 vs 1.23 mg/kg/day** (**P = 0.028**).
  - **NUDT15 rs116855232:** The **AZA dose-to-6-TGN ratio** was lower in **CT** than **CC** patients, indicating less dose was needed to reach metabolite exposure (**P = 0.001**).

- **Treatment efficacy:** NUDT15 affected toxicity and dose tolerance, but not observed remission outcomes in this study.
  - **NUDT15 rs116855232:** No significant difference was seen between **CC** and **CT** in the proportion achieving biochemical remission (**P = 0.361**).
  - **NUDT15 rs116855232:** No significant difference was seen in median time to biochemical remission (**3.0 vs 4.0 months; P = 0.161**).

- **TPMT findings:** TPMT*3C was not a useful predictor in this cohort.
  - **TPMT rs1142345 (*3C):** No significant association was found with AZA-induced leukopenia (**P = 0.283**).
  - **TPMT rs1142345 (*3C):** The variant was rare in this population (detected in only 4 patients total), limiting its predictive value here.

- **Clinical subgroup context:** Baseline leukopenia did not necessarily worsen after AZA.
  - **NUDT15 rs116855232:** In 13 patients who already had leukopenia before AZA, white blood cell counts did not show further reduction after treatment; only 1 patient in this subgroup carried **CT**, and that patient did not develop progressive leukopenia.
