## Summary

## Background
This retrospective study evaluated whether **UGT1A1 genetic variation** is linked to adverse effects from **nanoliposomal irinotecan plus 5-fluorouracil and L-leucovorin (nal-IRI/FL)** in **36 Japanese patients with metastatic pancreatic cancer**. Patients were grouped as **wild-type (–/–)**, **heterozygous (+/–)**, or **homozygous/compound heterozygous (+/+)** for **UGT1A1*6 or *28**. The main outcomes were diarrhea and blood count nadirs during treatment.

## Key Findings
- **Hematologic toxicity:** Homozygous or compound heterozygous **UGT1A1*6/*28-risk genotypes (+/+)** were associated with greater myelosuppression during nal-IRI/FL.
  - **UGT1A1*6:** Patients in the combined **UGT1A1*6 or *28 (+/+)** group had significantly lower white blood cell nadirs than the wild-type and heterozygous groups (**p=0.033**). In multiple regression, the **(+/+) genotype** remained significantly associated with decreased white blood cell count (**p=0.009**).
  - **UGT1A1*6:** The same **(+/+) group** also had significantly lower neutrophil nadirs (**p=0.043**), and the association with decreased neutrophil count remained significant in multiple regression (**p=0.017**).
  - **UGT1A1*28:** Because the analysis combined **UGT1A1*6 and *28** as a shared risk-genotype group, **UGT1A1*28/*28** was likewise included among the genotypes associated with lower white blood cell nadirs (**p=0.033**) and lower neutrophil nadirs (**p=0.043**); the combined **(+/+) genotype** was significant in regression for white blood cells (**p=0.009**) and neutrophils (**p=0.017**).
  - **Genotype details:** Among the 4 patients in the **(+/+)** group, genotypes were **UGT1A1*6/*6 (n=1)**, **UGT1A1*6/*28 (n=1)**, and **UGT1A1*28/*28 (n=2)**.

- **Dose selection in clinical practice:** Patients with higher-risk **UGT1A1** genotypes started nal-IRI at lower doses.
  - **UGT1A1*6 / UGT1A1*28:** Median initial nal-IRI dose was lower in the **UGT1A1*6 or *28 (+/+)** group than in the other genotype groups (**48 mg/m²** vs **56 mg/m²**; **p=0.011**).

- **Diarrhea risk:** No significant pharmacogenomic association with diarrhea was identified in this cohort.
  - **UGT1A1*6 / UGT1A1*28:** The incidence of grade ≥1 diarrhea was similar across genotype groups—**27.3%** in wild-type, **21.4%** in heterozygotes, and **25.0%** in the **(+/+)** group—and multivariate analysis found **no significant risk factor**, including UGT1A1 genotype.
