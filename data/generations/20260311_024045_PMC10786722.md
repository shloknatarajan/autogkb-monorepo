# Integrating rare genetic variants into DPYD pharmacogenetic testing may help preventing fluoropyrimidine-induced toxicity

**PMCID:** PMC10786722  
**PMID:** 38216550

## Summary

## Background
This study evaluated **DPYD** variation in **2,972 patients** eligible for fluoropyrimidine chemotherapy (such as 5-FU/capecitabine). The investigators used **next-generation sequencing of all DPYD exons and flanking splice sites** and compared genotypes with a pretreatment marker of DPD enzyme activity, the **plasma dihydrouracil/uracil ([UH2]/[U]) ratio**. **Partial DPD deficiency** was defined as **[UH2]/[U] ≤ 10**; 580 patients (19.7%) met this definition. The study did **not** include actual fluoropyrimidine toxicity outcomes, so the main pharmacogenomic endpoint was **reduced DPD activity**, a known risk factor for fluoropyrimidine toxicity.

## Key Findings
The clearest signals were for the established actionable **DPYD** deficiency variants: **c.1905+1G>A (DPYD*2A, rs3918290)**, **c.1679T>G (DPYD*13, rs55886062)**, and **c.2846A>T (rs67376798)**. Each showed a **significant association with lower DPD activity** in this cohort (**P < 0.001 to P < 0.0001**). Among common variants, **c.1236G>A / hapB3 (rs56038477)**, **c.496A>G (rs2297595)**, and **c.2194G>A / DPYD*6 (rs1801160)** were also associated with lower DPD activity (**P < 0.05 to P < 0.01**), but the effect was described as **more modest** than for the established rare deficiency variants. Other common variants evaluated, including **rs1801159, rs1801158, rs17376848, and rs1801265**, were **not significantly associated** with decreased DPD activity.

A major finding was the contribution of **rare DPYD variants**. Variants with **minor allele frequency <1%** were more common in patients with low DPD activity than in those with normal activity (**9.3% vs 3.2%; P < 0.00001**). Even after excluding the 3 established rare actionable variants above, rare variants remained enriched in the low-activity group (**4.5% vs 2.6%; P < 0.03**). When restricted to rare variants also predicted to be damaging (**CADD >15**), enrichment was stronger (**4.2% vs 1.6%; P < 0.001**). Examples of rare variants found only or mainly in patients with low DPD activity included **rs141044036 (c.2872A>G, p.Lys958Glu)**, **rs368146607 (c.1846A>C, p.Lys616Gln)**, **rs138616379 (c.1775G>A, p.Arg592Gln)**, **rs72549308 (c.601A>C, p.Ser201Arg)**, and **rs539032572 (DPYD*7, c.299_302del)**. Overall, the results show that **rare DPYD variants account for a meaningful share of reduced DPD activity beyond standard targeted genotyping**.
