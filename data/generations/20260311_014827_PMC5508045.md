# The impact of non-genetic and genetic factors on a stable warfarin dose in Thai patients

## Summary

## Background
This study evaluated why Thai patients need different stable doses of warfarin. The authors analyzed 250 adults on stable warfarin therapy and genotyped 4 variants using TaqMan assays: **VKORC1 -1639G>A (rs9923231)**, **CYP2C9*3 (rs1057910)**, **CYP4F2 rs2108622**, and **UGT1A1 rs887829**. The outcome was stable weekly warfarin dose (SWD).

## Key Findings
The strongest pharmacogenomic signals were seen for **VKORC1 -1639G>A** and **CYP2C9*3**. Patients with **VKORC1 GA** and **AA** genotypes required substantially lower warfarin doses than **GG** carriers: mean SWD was **27.9 mg/week** for GA and **19.4 mg/week** for AA versus **38.6 mg/week** for GG (**p < 0.001**). In multivariable analysis, compared with GG, the dose was reduced by **9.7 mg/week** for GA and **18.1 mg/week** for AA (both **p < 0.001**). For **CYP2C9*3**, patients with **\*1/\*3** and **\*3/\*3** also required lower doses than **\*1/\*1**: mean SWD was **14.9 mg/week** and **8.0 mg/week**, respectively, versus **22.6 mg/week** for \*1/\*1 (**p = 0.006**). In the regression model, these genotypes were associated with dose reductions of **7.9 mg/week** and **25.9 mg/week**, respectively (both **p < 0.001**).

**CYP4F2 rs2108622** did not show a significant genotype-dose association in univariate analysis (**p = 0.172**), but in multivariable analysis the **TT genotype** was associated with a **higher** warfarin requirement, increasing dose by **4.1 mg/week** versus **CC** (**p = 0.016**), accounting for about **1.2%** of dose variability. **UGT1A1 rs887829** was **not associated** with stable warfarin dose (**p = 0.921**).

Overall, the final multivariable model showed that **VKORC1, CYP2C9*3, and CYP4F2**, together with clinical factors, explained **51.3%** of variability in stable warfarin dose. Among the genetic factors, **VKORC1 -1639G>A** had the largest contribution, followed by **CYP2C9*3**, while **CYP4F2 rs2108622** had a modest effect and **UGT1A1 rs887829** had no detectable effect in this cohort.
