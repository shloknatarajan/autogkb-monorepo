# Susceptibility to thiopurine toxicity by TPMT and NUDT15 variants in Colombian children with acute lymphoblastic leukemia

## Summary

## Background
This study evaluated whether common thiopurine pharmacogenetic variants in TPMT and NUDT15 were associated with toxicity from 6-mercaptopurine (6-MP) during maintenance therapy in Colombian children with acute lymphoblastic leukemia (ALL). Genotyping was performed by TaqMan PCR, and patients were followed monthly for 6 months for myelotoxicity, liver toxicity, and 6-MP dose reduction or interruption.

## Key Findings
- **Variant frequencies in this Colombian pediatric ALL cohort:** Heterozygous risk variants were present, but no patients were homozygous for any variant tested.
  - Among 68 genotyped patients, 4 carried NUDT15 rs116855232 C/T (5.9%) and 64 had C/C; no T/T genotype was observed.
  - For TPMT, 4/68 were heterozygous for rs1800462, 6/68 for rs1800460, and 4/42 for rs1142345. In the 42 patients with complete TPMT typing, diplotypes were 36 with *1/*1, 2 with *1/*2, and 4 with *1/*3A.

- **Primary pharmacogenomic association results:** The study did not find statistically significant associations between NUDT15 or TPMT variants and thiopurine-related toxicity outcomes.
  - Individual SNP analyses showed no significant association between rs116855232, rs1800462, rs1800460, or rs1142345 and 6-MP dose reduction, 6-MP interruption, neutropenia, relapse, or death; the paper reports no significant p-values (threshold p < 0.05).
  - TPMT diplotype analysis also showed no significant association when comparing *1/*1 versus carriers of *1/*2 or *1/*3A for 6-MP reduction, interruption, neutropenia, relapse, or death (supplementary table p-values: 0.616, 0.308, 1.000, 0.557, and 1.000, respectively).

- **Observed clinical patterns without statistical significance:** Some heterozygous carriers had toxicity-related events, but numbers were small and did not support a significant genetic association.
  - Of the 4 patients with NUDT15 rs116855232 C/T, 2 required 6-MP dose reduction during follow-up.
  - Of the 4 patients heterozygous for TPMT rs1800462, 2 had febrile neutropenia and those same 2 required 6-MP interruption; among 6 patients heterozygous for rs1800460, 3 required dose reduction.

- **Laboratory toxicity markers:** Genotype was not associated with differences in routine laboratory measures during maintenance therapy.
  - Comparisons of ALT, AST, total bilirubin, white blood cell count, and differential counts across genotypes showed no statistically significant differences during the first 6 months of treatment.

- **Clinical outcome context:** Toxicity events were common in the cohort, but they were not linked to the tested pharmacogenetic markers in this study.
  - Across the full cohort, myelotoxicity occurred in 25%, liver toxicity in 27%, 6-MP reduction in 32.9%, and 6-MP interruption in 28.6%, yet none of these outcomes showed a significant association with the studied TPMT or NUDT15 variants.
