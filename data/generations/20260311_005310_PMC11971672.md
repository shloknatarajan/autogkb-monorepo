# Cytochrome P450 2C19 Genotypes and Clopidogrel in Patients With Ischemic Stroke: A Nonrandomized Clinical Trial

## Summary

## Background
This prospective, multicenter, nonrandomized clinical trial studied 2,910 South Korean patients with acute ischemic stroke who were treated with clopidogrel within 72 hours of stroke onset. Patients were genotyped for *CYP2C19* *1, *2, *3,* and *17* alleles and grouped as either loss-of-function (LOF) allele carriers or noncarriers. LOF carriers included intermediate and poor metabolizers with genotypes *1/*2, *1/*3, *2/*17, *3/*17, *2/*2, *2/*3, and *3/*3; noncarriers (extensive metabolizers) had *1/*1, *1/*17, or *17/*17.

## Key Findings
Carriers of *CYP2C19* LOF alleles had worse clinical outcomes on clopidogrel than noncarriers. The primary composite cardiovascular outcome at 6 months (stroke, myocardial infarction, or cardiovascular death) occurred in 2.7% of LOF carriers versus 1.6% of noncarriers (log-rank *P* = .048). In Cox analysis, LOF noncarriers had a lower risk than carriers (HR, 0.58; 95% CI, 0.34-0.99; adjusted HR, 0.58; 95% CI, 0.33-0.99). Recurrent stroke was also more common in LOF carriers (2.5% vs 1.4%; HR, 0.56; 95% CI, 0.32-1.00; *P* = .047).

The main variants driving the LOF carrier group were *CYP2C19* *2* and *3*, while *1/*1, *1/*17, and *17/*17 were part of the noncarrier group associated with better clopidogrel outcomes. There were no significant genotype-related differences in major bleeding (0.6% in carriers vs 0.8% in noncarriers; HR, 1.29; 95% CI, 0.53-3.13; *P* = .56), all-cause mortality (0.3% vs 0.1%; *P* = .27), or ischemic stroke considered separately (2.4% vs 1.4%; *P* = .06). Among LOF carriers, intermediate metabolizers did not differ significantly from poor metabolizers for the primary outcome (HR, 0.96; 95% CI, 0.49-1.88; *P* = .92), including in patients receiving aspirin plus clopidogrel (HR, 0.99; 95% CI, 0.50-1.93; *P* = .98).
