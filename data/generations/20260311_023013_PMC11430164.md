## Summary

## Background
This in vitro pharmacogenomics study examined how **22 CYP3A4 variants found in the Chinese Han population** affect the metabolism of **ticagrelor**, an antiplatelet drug. Recombinant CYP3A4 proteins expressed in insect cells were incubated with ticagrelor (1–50 µM), and formation of its active metabolite **AR-C124910XX** was measured by **UHPLC-MS/MS**. The main readout was **intrinsic clearance (CLint = Vmax/Km)** compared with wild-type **CYP3A4*1**.

## Key Findings
Wild-type **CYP3A4*1** showed a CLint of **800.45 µL/min/nmol**. Most variants significantly altered ticagrelor metabolism. Three variants had **significantly higher intrinsic clearance** than wild type (**p < 0.01**): **CYP3A4*11** (**264.73%** of wild type; CLint 2123.33), **CYP3A4*18** (**122.49%**; CLint 977.79), and **CYP3A4*33** (**117.77%**; CLint 940.29). Four variants showed **no significant difference** from wild type: **CYP3A4*10** (111.42%), **CYP3A4*23** (92.82%), **CYP3A4*32** (103.77%), and **CYP3A4*34** (111.93%).

The remaining **14 variants had significantly reduced intrinsic clearance** versus wild type (**p < 0.01**), indicating slower ticagrelor metabolism: **CYP3A4*2** (25.52%), ***3** (35.01%), ***4** (68.84%), ***5** (46.25%), ***9** (74.97%), ***14** (28.74%), ***15** (71.80%), ***16** (45.07%), ***17** (4.72%), ***19** (67.37%), ***24** (1.48%), ***28** (53.32%), ***29** (66.46%), and ***31** (75.11%). Among these, **CYP3A4*17** and **CYP3A4*24** showed the most marked loss of function. **CYP3A4*30** had **no detectable activity** toward ticagrelor, so kinetic parameters could not be determined.

Overall, the study shows that **ticagrelor metabolism is strongly allele-dependent for CYP3A4**, with variants ranging from **complete or near-complete loss of activity** to **substantially increased clearance**. These findings support CYP3A4 genotype as an important determinant of ticagrelor metabolic variability in this experimental system.
