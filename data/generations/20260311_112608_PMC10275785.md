# Effect of NLRP3 inflammasome genes polymorphism on disease susceptibility and response to TNF-α inhibitors in Iraqi patients with rheumatoid arthritis

## Summary

## Background
This case-control study evaluated whether two inflammasome-related variants, **CARD8 rs2043211** and **NLRP3 rs4612666**, are associated with rheumatoid arthritis (RA) susceptibility, disease activity, and response to **TNF-α inhibitors** (**etanercept** or **infliximab**) in **100 Iraqi RA patients** and **100 healthy controls**. Genotyping was performed by SYBR Green real-time PCR, and treatment response was assessed after 3 months using **DAS-28/EULAR-based criteria**.

## Key Findings
- **Disease susceptibility:** Neither variant was significantly associated with overall risk of RA in this cohort.
  - **CARD8 rs2043211:** Genotype distribution did not differ between RA cases and controls (**P = 0.17**); allele frequencies were also not significantly different (**P = 0.059**).
  - **NLRP3 rs4612666:** Genotype distribution was not significantly different between cases and controls (**P = 0.08**); allele frequencies were similar (**P = 0.879**).

- **Response to TNF-α inhibitors:** Variant genotypes were associated with poorer clinical response after 3 months of etanercept or infliximab treatment.
  - **CARD8 rs2043211:** Non-response was much more common in **TT** carriers than in **AA** carriers; among responders/non-responders, counts were **AA: 32/5**, **AT: 33/14**, **TT: 1/15** (**P = 0.0001**). In model-based analysis, **T-allele carriers (AT+TT)** had lower response than **AA** (**P = 0.001**), while **AA+AT vs TT** was also strongly associated with better response (**P < 0.0001**; reported OR **0.019**, 95% CI **0.002–0.157**).
  - **NLRP3 rs4612666:** Non-response was more common in **TT** carriers than in **CC** carriers; responder/non-responder counts were **CC: 41/6**, **CT: 24/18**, **TT: 1/10** (**P = 0.0001**). **T-allele carriers (CT+TT)** had poorer response than **CC** (**P < 0.0001**), and **CC+CT vs TT** also favored response (**P = 0.001**; reported OR **0.183**, 95% CI **0.063–0.531**; additional model OR reported **0.131**, 95% CI **0.047–0.36**).

- **Disease activity during treatment:** The same variants were linked to higher residual disease activity after TNF-α inhibitor therapy.
  - **CARD8 rs2043211:** Mean DAS-28 increased across genotypes: **AA 3.51 ± 0.90**, **AT 3.92 ± 1.00**, **TT 4.65 ± 0.65** (**P < 0.0001**). T-allele carriers (**AT+TT**) also had higher disease activity than **AA** in genetic model analysis (**P = 0.029**).
  - **NLRP3 rs4612666:** Mean DAS-28 also increased across genotypes: **CC 3.52 ± 0.89**, **CT 4.08 ± 1.00**, **TT 4.72 ± 0.55** (**P < 0.0001**). **CT+TT** was associated with higher disease activity versus **CC** (**P = 0.002**).

- **Inflammatory biomarker context:** Poor-response genotypes were also associated with higher inflammatory cytokine levels after treatment.
  - **CARD8 rs2043211:** Mean **TNF-α** levels were **5.64 ± 3.56 pg/mL (AA)**, **7.39 ± 5.59 (AT)**, and **14.25 ± 4.67 (TT)**; mean **IL-1β** levels were **1.05 ± 0.34**, **2.25 ± 0.57**, and **4.55 ± 0.64**, respectively (**P < 0.0001** for both). Dominant-model analysis showed lower cytokine levels in **AA+AT** versus **TT** (**P < 0.0001** for TNF-α and IL-1β).
  - **NLRP3 rs4612666:** Mean **TNF-α** levels were **5.56 ± 3.31 pg/mL (CC)**, **8.58 ± 6.17 (CT)**, and **14.74 ± 4.61 (TT)**; mean **IL-1β** levels were **1.18 ± 0.39**, **2.59 ± 0.69**, and **4.86 ± 0.50**, respectively (**P < 0.0001** for both). **CT+TT** was associated with higher TNF-α (**P = 0.002**) and IL-1β (**P < 0.0001**) versus **CC**.

- **Clinical biomarker-response relationship:** Cytokine levels tracked with treatment outcome and disease activity, supporting their use as response-associated markers in this cohort.
  - **TNF-α:** Positively correlated with DAS-28 (**r² = 0.45, P < 0.0001**); levels were higher in non-responders than responders (**13.68 ± 5.50 vs 4.83 ± 2.18 pg/mL, P < 0.0001**).
  - **IL-1β:** Positively correlated with DAS-28 (**r² = 0.51, P < 0.0001**); levels were higher in non-responders than responders (**3.36 ± 1.24 vs 1.57 ± 0.58 pg/mL, P < 0.0001**).

Overall, in this Iraqi RA cohort, **CARD8 rs2043211 T** and **NLRP3 rs4612666 T** were **not RA susceptibility markers**, but were associated with **higher inflammation, higher post-treatment disease activity, and poorer response to TNF-α inhibitors**.
