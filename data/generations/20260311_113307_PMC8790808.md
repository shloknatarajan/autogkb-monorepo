## Summary

## Background
This study examined germline genetic predictors of **pegaspargase hypersensitivity** in children and adolescents with acute lymphoblastic leukemia treated on three frontline protocols. The analysis included genome-wide SNP testing and HLA-focused analyses across ancestries, with the main outcome defined as **grade 2 or higher hypersensitivity reactions** to pegaspargase.

## Key Findings
- **Class II HLA risk alleles in European-ancestry patients:** The strongest and most consistent pharmacogenomic signal was in the **class II HLA region** among patients with primarily European ancestry.
  - **HLA-DQB1*02:02:** Strongest HLA association with higher risk of pegaspargase hypersensitivity in European-ancestry patients; **P_meta = 3.76×10^-9, OR 2.14**.
  - **HLA-DRB1*07:01:** Also associated with increased risk; **P_meta = 2.22×10^-5, OR 1.99**.
  - **HLA-DQA1*02:01:** Associated with increased risk; **P_meta = 2.74×10^-5, OR 1.99**.
  - These 3 alleles occurred together as a common haplotype (**HLA-DRB1*07:01–DQA1*02:01–DQB1*02:02**), indicating that the risk signal reflects linked class II HLA variation.

- **Genome-wide significant SNPs in the HLA region:** In genome-wide analyses, the significant SNP findings in European-ancestry patients were all in the same class II HLA region and tracked the HLA risk haplotype.
  - **rs1694129 (near HLA-DQB1):** Top SNP in European-ancestry patients; **P_meta = 1.1×10^-8, OR 2.03**. It strongly tagged **HLA-DQB1*02:02** in Europeans (**r^2 = 0.96**).
  - **rs28383308 (near HLA-DQA1):** Top SNP in the all-ancestry meta-analysis; **P_meta = 4.9×10^-14, OR 1.95**. In Europeans, it tagged **HLA-DRB1*07:01** (**r^2 = 0.85**) and, to a lesser extent, **HLA-DQB1*02:02** (**r^2 = 0.62**).
  - After adjusting for **HLA-DQB1*02:02** or **HLA-DRB1*07:01**, no additional genome-wide significant SNPs remained in Europeans, supporting that these SNP signals largely reflect the underlying HLA haplotype.

- **HLA amino acid signal:** Amino acid analysis pointed to the same main HLA risk effect.
  - **Glycine at position 135 of HLA-DQB1:** Strongest amino acid association with hypersensitivity; **P_meta = 3.8×10^-9, OR 2.13**. This residue marks **HLA-DQB1*02:02**.

- **Non-European ancestry findings:** The HLA signal was weaker and less consistent outside European-ancestry groups, but some associations were still seen.
  - **HLA-DRB1*07:01:** Nominal association with increased risk in non-European patients; **P_meta = 0.011, OR 1.94**.
  - **HLA-DQA1*02:01:** Nominal association with increased risk; **P_meta = 0.024, OR 2.09**.
  - **HLA-DQB1*02:02:** Nominal association with increased risk; **P_meta = 0.041, OR 1.75**.
  - **rs1694129:** Also showed a nominal association in non-European patients; **P_meta = 0.013, OR 2.01**, but tagging of the HLA risk alleles was weaker than in Europeans.

- **Non-HLA variants in non-European patients:** The main non-HLA pharmacogenomic signal was seen in non-European patients.
  - **rs9958628 (ARHGAP28):** Strongest non-HLA association in non-European patients; **P_meta = 8.9×10^-9, OR 3.69**. It was also the top non-HLA hit in the full cohort (**P_meta = 1.2×10^-7, OR 3.03**).
  - **rs9958628 in African-ancestry patients:** Associated with higher risk in African-ancestry meta-analysis; **P_meta = 3.2×10^-5, OR 2.03**.
  - **rs79377225 and rs11739459 (near GPBP1):** Both were associated with higher risk in non-European patients at genome-wide significance; **rs79377225: P_meta = 2.5×10^-8, OR 2.70** and **rs11739459: P_meta = 2.7×10^-8, OR 2.50**.
  - **Trans-ancestry meta-analysis:** **rs11739459** and **rs79377225** remained genome-wide significant across ancestry-specific analyses (**P_meta = 8.8×10^-9, OR 2.60** and **P_meta = 1.6×10^-8, OR 2.74**, respectively).

- **Other HLA alleles in Europeans:** Additional HLA alleles showed associations in European-ancestry meta-analysis, but the clearest signals were the class II alleles above.
  - **HLA-DRB1*04:02:** Increased risk; **P_meta = 6.30×10^-5, OR 4.49**.
  - **HLA-DRB1*04:05:** Increased risk; **P_meta = 5.44×10^-4, OR 3.75**.
