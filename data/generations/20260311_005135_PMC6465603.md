## Summary

## Background
This retrospective study evaluated 149 Chinese patients with autoimmune hepatitis (AIH) or AIH–primary biliary cholangitis variant syndrome who had received azathioprine (AZA) for more than 3 months. The researchers genotyped **NUDT15 rs116855232 (R139C)** and **TPMT*3C rs1142345** by PCR/Sanger sequencing and examined associations with AZA-induced leukopenia, AZA metabolite levels, and maintenance dose.

## Key Findings
The main pharmacogenomic signal was **NUDT15 rs116855232**. AZA-induced leukopenia occurred in **12/149 patients (8.1%)** and was strongly associated with the **T risk allele**: **OR 20.41 (95% CI 7.84–53.13), P < 0.00001**. By genotype, leukopenia developed in **1/123 (0.8%)** patients with **CC**, **9/24 (37.5%)** with **CT**, and **2/2 (100%)** with **TT**. Under a dominant model (**CT+TT vs CC**), the association was very strong: **OR 89.47 (95% CI 10.78–742.53), P < 0.0001**. All leukopenia cases occurred within the first 60 days after starting AZA or increasing the dose. The reported sensitivity and specificity of the **NUDT15 T allele** for leukopenia were **91.67%** and **89.05%**, respectively.

NUDT15 variation was also linked to AZA pharmacology. Among patients with metabolite testing, those with **NUDT15 CT** had significantly higher **6-thioguanine nucleotide (6-TGN)** levels than **CC** carriers: **276.6 vs 145.3 pmol/8×10^8 RBC, P = 0.002**. In patients who reached biochemical remission, **CT** carriers required a lower AZA maintenance dose than **CC** carriers: **0.96 vs 1.23 mg/kg/day, P = 0.028**. The AZA dose-to-6-TGN ratio was also lower in **CT** carriers (**P = 0.001–0.013**, depending on the comparison reported). In contrast, **TPMT*3C rs1142345** was rare and **not significantly associated with leukopenia** (**P = 0.283**). **NUDT15 genotype was not associated with remission rate or time to remission** (**P = 0.361** and **P = 0.161**, respectively).
