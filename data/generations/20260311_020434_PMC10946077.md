## Summary

## Background
This retrospective study evaluated whether pre-treatment **UGT1A1** genotyping could help guide **irinotecan** use in 110 patients with advanced gastric cancer receiving second-line therapy. Irinotecan dose was adjusted by **UGT1A1*6** genotype: **GG 125 mg/m²**, **GA 100 mg/m²**, and **AA 75 mg/m²**; some patients with **AA** were switched to **paclitaxel**. The study also assessed **UGT1A1*28** genotypes, with outcomes including toxicity, progression-free survival (PFS), and overall survival (OS).

## Key Findings
The main pharmacogenomic signal was for **UGT1A1*6**, not **UGT1A1*28**. **UGT1A1*6** genotype was significantly associated with irinotecan toxicity: rates of **delayed diarrhea** (**p=0.000**), **neutropenia** (**p=0.000**), and **leukopenia** (**p=0.003**) differed across **GG, GA, and AA** groups. Toxicity was especially severe in **AA homozygotes**: grade 3/4 diarrhea, neutropenia, and leukopenia occurred in **100% (5/5 treated with irinotecan)** of AA patients in Table 3, compared with much lower rates in **GG** patients (**2%, 24%, 19%**) and **GA** patients (**23%, 31%, 31%**). In practice, **5 of 8 AA patients** developed severe toxicity and were switched to paclitaxel, while **3 additional AA patients** received paclitaxel directly.

By contrast, **UGT1A1*28** was **not significantly associated** with irinotecan toxicity in this cohort: diarrhea (**p=0.136**), neutropenia (**p=0.991**), and leukopenia (**p=0.857**) did not differ across *28 genotype groups. Neither **UGT1A1*6** nor **UGT1A1*28** was associated with survival outcomes after genotype-guided treatment adjustment. For **UGT1A1*6**, GA+AA versus GG showed no association with **PFS** in univariate analysis (**HR 1.290, 95% CI 0.811-2.052, p=0.283**) or multivariable analysis (**HR 1.325, 95% CI 0.792-2.219, p=0.284**), and no association with **OS** in univariate (**HR 1.016, 95% CI 0.610-1.692, p=0.951**) or multivariable analysis (**HR 0.894, 95% CI 0.504-1.588, p=0.703**). Similarly, **UGT1A1*28** variant carriers (TA6/TA7 + TA7/TA7) had no significant differences in **PFS** or **OS** versus TA6/TA6. Overall, the Results support **UGT1A1*6** as a predictor of irinotecan toxicity in this gastric cancer population, while **UGT1A1*28** did not show a clear effect.
