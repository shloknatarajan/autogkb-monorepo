## Summary

## Background
This study analyzed pharmacogenomic predictors of response to efavirenz (EFV) 400 mg vs 600 mg once daily in 606 treatment-naïve adults with HIV over 96 weeks in the ENCORE1 trial. Patients were genotyped for variants in **CYP2B6, CYP2A6, CYP3A4, NR1I3, NR1I2,** and **ABCB1**, and the investigators tested associations with virologic suppression, EFV discontinuation, and adverse events.

## Key Findings
Overall, **none of the tested genetic variants were associated with virologic success** at week 96 (plasma HIV RNA <200 copies/mL). This included common EFV metabolism variants such as **CYP2B6 516G>T (rs3745274)**, **CYP2B6 983T>C (rs28399499)**, **CYP2B6 15582C>T (rs4803419)**, **CYP2A6*9B (rs8192726)**, **CYP2A6*17 (rs28399454)**, **CYP3A4*22 (rs35599367)**, **ABCB1 3435C>T (rs1045642)**, and **NR1I2/NR1I3** variants (all non-significant; e.g., CYP2B6 516G>T p=0.420, ABCB1 3435C>T p=0.797).

The clearest pharmacogenomic signals were for **safety**, especially CNS toxicity. **CYP2B6 983TC/CC** was associated with a **lower risk of CNS adverse events** compared with TT carriers (OR 0.30, 95% CI 0.12–0.75, p=0.010; similar crude estimate OR 0.35, p=0.015). In contrast, **CYP2B6 15582CT/TT** was associated with a **higher risk of CNS adverse events** (OR 1.59, 95% CI 1.11–2.27, p=0.011; crude OR 1.46, p=0.040), and **ABCB1 3435TT** also increased CNS adverse event risk (OR 2.14, 95% CI 1.25–3.67, p=0.006; crude OR 2.31, p=0.003).

For **overall EFV discontinuation**, adjusted Cox models showed increased risk in carriers of **CYP2B6 516GT** (HR 1.80, 95% CI 1.01–3.21, p=0.047) and **516TT** (HR 2.66, 95% CI 1.26–5.60, p=0.010), as well as **CYP2A6*9B CA/AA** (HR 2.00, 95% CI 1.14–3.52, p=0.016). **NR1I2 63396TT (rs2472677)** was associated with a **lower risk of discontinuation** (HR 0.22, 95% CI 0.07–0.67, p=0.008). Other tested variants, including **CYP2A6*17**, **CYP3A4*22**, **NR1I3 540C>T**, **NR1I3 1089T>C**, and **NR1I2 7635A>G**, were not significantly associated with virologic outcome, and most were not retained as independent predictors of discontinuation.
