## Summary

## Background
This study evaluated whether common pharmacokinetic gene variants affect steady-state simvastatin exposure in 89 Thai patients with dyslipidemia or coronary artery disease who had been taking simvastatin for at least 2 weeks without dose changes. Blood was drawn about 12 hours after the last dose, and plasma simvastatin lactone (inactive form) and simvastatin acid (active form) were measured by UHPLC–MS/MS. Ten variants in seven genes were genotyped: *ABCB1, ABCC2, ABCG2, SLCO1B1, SLCO1B3, CYP3A4,* and *CYP3A5*.

## Key Findings
The main pharmacogenomic signal was in *SLCO1B1*, which encodes the OATP1B1 hepatic uptake transporter. Patients carrying the *SLCO1B1* rs4149056 c.521C allele (TC+CC) had significantly higher dose-normalized simvastatin acid concentrations than TT carriers: median 0.53 vs 0.19 ng/mL (*p* = 0.03). This association was seen for simvastatin acid, not simvastatin lactone (*p* = 0.33). At the diplotype level, *SLCO1B1* *1b/*15 was associated with markedly higher simvastatin acid concentrations than *1a/*1a (0.58 vs 0.16 ng/mL, *p* < 0.001). When grouped by predicted OATP1B1 function, decreased-function diplotypes (*1b/*5 or *1b/*15) showed higher simvastatin acid levels than normal-function diplotypes, but this did not reach statistical significance (*p* = 0.07).

For *SLCO1B1* rs2306283 c.388A>G, there was no significant overall association with simvastatin acid or lactone concentrations in the full cohort (simvastatin acid *p* = 0.13; lactone *p* = 0.53). However, in the 10 mg/day subgroup, carriers of the G allele (AG+GG) had higher simvastatin acid concentrations than AA carriers (3.63 vs 1.59 ng/mL, *p* = 0.04). In the same dose subgroup, rs4149056 TC+CC carriers also had higher simvastatin acid than TT carriers (5.83 vs 1.95 ng/mL), although this was only borderline significant (*p* = 0.06).

No significant associations were found between simvastatin plasma concentrations and variants in *ABCB1* rs1045642, *ABCC2* rs717620 or rs3740066, *ABCG2* rs2231142, *SLCO1B3* rs7311158 or rs4149117, *CYP3A4* rs2242480, or *CYP3A5* rs776746. Overall, the results support *SLCO1B1* rs4149056—and especially the *SLCO1B1* *1b/*15 diplotype—as the key genetic predictors of higher simvastatin acid exposure in this Thai cohort.
