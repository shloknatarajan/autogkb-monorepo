## Summary

## Background
This preliminary pharmacogenetic study evaluated escitalopram in 66 adolescents (ages 12–17) with depression and/or anxiety who also had a first-degree relative with bipolar I disorder. Participants underwent pharmacogenetic testing for **CYP2C19**, **CYP2D6**, **HTR2A** (rs6311, −1438G>A), and **SLC6A4**, and a subset (n=48) also had serum escitalopram levels measured to estimate pharmacokinetic exposure.

## Key Findings
**CYP2C19 phenotype affected escitalopram pharmacokinetics, but not adverse events.** Slower **CYP2C19** metabolizers had significantly higher dose-normalized escitalopram exposure, including higher **AUC0–24** (*p* = 0.025), higher **trough concentrations** (*p* = 0.013), and longer **half-life** (*p* < 0.001). There was also a trend toward higher **Cmax** in slower metabolizers (*p* = 0.057). However, **CYP2C19 phenotype was not significantly associated with clinical adverse events**, including hyperarousal, PAERS scores, or TEASAP subscales.

**CYP2D6 phenotype did not affect escitalopram pharmacokinetics, but was linked to activation-related side effects.** **CYP2D6** phenotype was not associated with AUC, Cmax, trough concentration, or half-life (*p* = 0.48–0.56). Still, slower **CYP2D6** metabolizers had higher **TEASAP akathisia/hyperkinesis/somatic anxiety** scores (*p* = 0.015). In a grouped analysis, slower-than-normal **CYP2D6** metabolism was also associated with higher **disinhibition/impulsivity** scores (mean 3.0 vs 1.1, *p* = 0.02).

For pharmacodynamic genes, **HTR2A rs6311** showed the main signal: youth with **A/A or A/G genotypes** had greater increases in **TEASAP self-injury, suicidality, and harm to others** scores than those with **G/G** (*p* = 0.017). **HTR2A** was not significantly associated with other TEASAP subscales, PAERS score, or hyperarousal events. **SLC6A4** genotype (**S/S** vs **L/L + L/S**) was **not associated** with adverse-event measures or hyperarousal. Overall, the clearest pharmacogenomic findings were that **CYP2C19** influenced escitalopram exposure, while **CYP2D6** and **HTR2A rs6311** were associated with selected adverse-effect outcomes.
