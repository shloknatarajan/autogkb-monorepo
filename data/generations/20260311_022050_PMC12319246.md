# Pharmacogenetics association with long-term clinical evolution in a kidney transplant patients cohort

## Summary

## Background
This study examined whether pharmacogenetic variation affects long-term outcomes in 79 adult kidney transplant recipients treated with tacrolimus. Investigators genotyped 37 SNPs in 14 drug metabolism and transporter genes in both recipients and donors, then related these variants to 12-year outcomes including survival, tumor incidence, rejection, nephrotoxicity, renal function, infections, and tacrolimus dose-adjusted trough concentrations (C/D ratio).

## Key Findings
The strongest long-term survival associations involved donor transporter genes. Donor **ABCB1 rs9282564 AA** was linked to lower mortality risk over follow-up (**OR 0.116, p=0.029**), while donor **ABCC2 rs2273697 GA** was linked to higher mortality (**OR 4.133, p=0.021**); Kaplan–Meier analysis also showed worse survival for donor **ABCB1 rs9282564 AG** (**log-rank p=0.042**) and for donor **ABCC2 rs2273697 AA/GA** versus GG (**log-rank p=0.003**). For tumors, recipient **CYP2B6 rs3745274 GT** was associated with lower tumor risk (**OR 0.099, p=0.048**), whereas donor **ABCC2 rs2273697 AA** increased tumor risk (**OR 13.091, p=0.043**), with **GA** also showing a weaker increase (**OR 3.394, p=0.058**).

Several variants were associated with tacrolimus exposure and kidney-related outcomes. Recipient **CYP3A4 rs2740574 AA** was associated with both lower acute rejection risk (**OR 0.080, p=0.047**) and higher tacrolimus C/D ratios throughout follow-up (**p<0.001**). Recipient **CYP3A5 rs776746 GG** also showed higher tacrolimus C/D than GA (**p<0.001**). Donor **CYP2C19 rs4244285 AA** was associated with markedly higher tacrolimus C/D and increased nephrotoxicity risk (**OR 11.652, p=0.026**). In transporter genes, recipient **SLCO1B1 rs4149056 TT** was linked to reduced acute rejection (**OR 0.157, p=0.024**) and higher tacrolimus C/D (**p=0.002**). Recipient **SLCO1B1 rs2306283 AA** reduced nephrotoxicity risk (**OR 0.076, p=0.009**), and **AG** was also protective (**OR 0.227, post-FDR p=0.051**); donor **SLCO1B1 rs2306283 AG** was likewise protective (**OR 0.108, p=0.024**). Donor **ABCB1 rs2235013 GA** lowered chronic rejection risk (**OR 0.133, p=0.016**), and donor **GG** at this SNP was associated with lower tacrolimus C/D (**p=0.011**).

Renal function over time also differed by genotype. Better renal clearance was seen with recipient **CYP3A4 rs2745074 AA** versus GA (**p<0.001**), donor **CYP3A5 rs776746 GG** versus GA (**p<0.001**), recipient **CYP2B6 rs3745274 GT** versus TT (**p=0.018**), donor **CYP2B6 rs3745274 GT** versus TT (**p=0.036**), recipient **ABCB1 rs1045642 CC**, and donor **ABCC2 rs2273697 GG** (**p=0.039**). Additional significant clinical associations included lower infection risk with recipient **ABCB1 rs1045642 CC** (**OR 0.087, p=0.003**) but higher infection risk with donor **ABCB1 rs9282564 AA** (**OR 23.8, p=0.005**); lower re-transplantation risk with donor **TPMT rs1142345 AA** (**OR 0.048, p=0.020**) and donor **CYP2C9 rs1799853 CC** (**OR 0.102, p=0.042**). The study also found genotype-combination effects, including higher death risk when recipient **CYP3A5 rs776746 GA** co-occurred with donor **ABCB1 rs2235013 GA** or donor **SLCO1B1 rs2306283 AG** (**OR 6.4** each).
