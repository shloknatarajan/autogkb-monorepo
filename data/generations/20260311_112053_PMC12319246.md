# Pharmacogenetics association with long-term clinical evolution in a kidney transplant patients cohort

## Summary

## Background
This study evaluated pharmacogenomic predictors of long-term outcomes in 79 adult kidney transplant recipients treated with tacrolimus. The investigators genotyped 37 SNPs across 14 pharmacogenes in both recipients and donors, then assessed associations with 12-year survival, tumor incidence, renal outcomes, infections, and tacrolimus dose-adjusted trough concentrations (C/D ratio).

## Key Findings
- **Long-term survival:** Donor transporter gene variants were the strongest survival signals over 12 years.
  - **ABCB1 rs9282564 (donor):** AA genotype was associated with lower risk of death (OR 0.116, p=0.029 after FDR correction). Kaplan–Meier analysis also showed worse cumulative survival for donor AG vs AA (log-rank p=0.042).
  - **ABCC2 rs2273697 (donor):** GA genotype was associated with higher risk of death (OR 4.133, p=0.021 after FDR correction). Kaplan–Meier curves showed best survival with donor GG genotype (log-rank p=0.003).
  - **CYP2B6 rs3745274 (recipient):** GT genotype showed a possible protective association with death (OR 0.224, p=0.060), but this did not remain significant after FDR correction.
  - **CYP3A5 rs776746 (recipient):** GG (*3/*3) showed a possible protective association with death (OR 0.264, p=0.066), but this was not significant after FDR correction.

- **Tumor occurrence:** Both donor transport variants and recipient metabolism variants were linked to de novo tumor risk.
  - **ABCC2 rs2273697 (donor):** AA genotype markedly increased tumor risk (OR 13.091, p=0.043); GA also showed increased risk (OR 3.394, p=0.058).
  - **CYP2B6 rs3745274 (recipient):** GT genotype was associated with lower tumor risk (OR 0.099, p=0.048).
  - **ABCB1 rs9282564 (donor):** AA genotype showed a borderline protective association for tumor occurrence (OR 0.120, p=0.057).

- **Tacrolimus pharmacokinetics:** Several metabolism and transporter variants affected dose-adjusted tacrolimus exposure over time.
  - **CYP3A4 rs2740574 (recipient):** AA genotype was associated with higher tacrolimus C/D ratios throughout follow-up than GA (p<0.001).
  - **CYP3A5 rs776746 (recipient):** GG (*3/*3) was associated with higher tacrolimus C/D ratios than GA (*1/*3) (p<0.001).
  - **CYP2C19 rs4244285 (donor):** AA genotype was associated with higher tacrolimus C/D ratios than GG or GA, especially from year 3 onward.
  - **ABCB1 rs1045642 (recipient):** TT genotype was associated with higher tacrolimus C/D ratios (p=0.005).
  - **ABCB1 rs2235033 (recipient):** TT genotype was associated with higher tacrolimus C/D ratios (p=0.005).
  - **ABCB1 rs2235013 (donor):** GG genotype was associated with lower, more stable tacrolimus C/D ratios (p=0.011).
  - **SLCO1B1 rs4149056 (recipient):** TT genotype nearly doubled tacrolimus C/D ratio compared with TC (p=0.002).
  - **SLCO1B1 rs2306283 (donor):** AA vs GG showed significant early post-transplant differences in tacrolimus C/D (p=0.01).

- **Renal outcomes and nephrotoxicity:** Variants in drug metabolism and transport genes were associated with rejection, renal recovery, and tacrolimus-related nephrotoxicity.
  - **Acute rejection**
    - **CYP3A4 rs2740574 (recipient):** AA genotype was protective (OR 0.080, p=0.047).
    - **SLCO1B1 rs4149056 (recipient):** TT genotype was protective (OR 0.157, p=0.024).
    - **CYP2B6 rs3745274 (donor):** GT genotype was protective (OR 0.199, p=0.027).
    - **UGT1A9 rs6714486 (donor):** TA genotype was associated with increased risk (OR 32.116, p=0.020), though genotype imbalance was noted.
  - **Chronic rejection**
    - **ABCB1 rs2235013 (donor):** GA genotype was associated with lower risk (OR 0.133, p=0.016).
  - **Nephrotoxicity**
    - **SLCO1B1 rs2306283 (recipient):** AA genotype reduced risk (OR 0.076, p=0.009); AG also showed reduced risk (OR 0.227, p=0.051).
    - **SLCO1B1 rs2306283 (donor):** AG genotype reduced risk (OR 0.108, p=0.024).
    - **CYP2C19 rs4244285 (donor):** AA genotype strongly increased risk (OR 11.652, p=0.026).
    - **NOD2 rs2066844 (donor):** CC genotype reduced risk (OR 0.015, p=0.035).
  - **Renal function recovery (CKD-EPI)**
    - **CYP3A4 rs2740574/rs2745074 (recipient):** AA genotype was associated with better renal clearance recovery than GA (p<0.001).
    - **CYP3A5 rs776746 (donor):** GG (*3/*3) was associated with higher renal clearance than GA (*1/*3) (p<0.001).
    - **CYP2B6 rs3745274 (recipient):** GT genotype had higher renal clearance than TT (p=0.018).
    - **CYP2B6 rs3745274 (donor):** GT genotype had better renal clearance recovery than TT (p=0.036).
    - **ABCB1 rs1045642 (recipient):** CC genotype showed earlier and better renal clearance recovery than TC/TT.
    - **ABCC2 rs2273697 (donor):** GG genotype was associated with better renal clearance throughout follow-up (p=0.039).

- **Other clinical outcomes:** Some variants were associated with infection risk, diabetes, and re-transplantation.
  - **ABCB1 rs1045642 (recipient):** CC genotype was associated with lower infection risk (OR 0.087, p=0.003).
  - **ABCB1 rs9282564 (donor):** AA genotype was associated with higher infection risk (OR 23.800, p=0.005).
  - **CYP2B6 rs3745274 (recipient):** GT genotype showed a borderline lower risk of de novo diabetes (OR 0.273, p=0.056).
  - **TPMT rs1142345 (donor):** AA genotype was associated with lower risk of re-transplantation (OR 0.048, p=0.020).
  - **CYP2C9 rs1799853 (donor):** CC genotype was associated with lower risk of re-transplantation (OR 0.102, p=0.042).

- **Gene–gene interaction findings:** Combinations of metabolizer and transporter variants also altered risk.
  - **CYP3A5 rs776746 (recipient GA) + ABCB1 rs2235013 (donor GA):** Increased risk of death (OR 6.4).
  - **CYP3A5 rs776746 (recipient GA) + SLCO1B1 rs2306283 (donor AG):** Increased risk of death (OR 6.4).
  - **ABCC2 rs2273697 (donor GG) + CYP2C19 rs4244285 (donor AG or AA):** Reduced tumor risk (OR <0.2).
  - **CYP2B6 rs2279343 (recipient GA) + ABCC2 rs2273697 (donor GG):** Increased tumor risk (OR 5.3).
  - **CYP2B6 rs3745274 (recipient TT) + ABCB1 rs1045642 (recipient TC):** Increased acute rejection risk.
  - **CYP2B6 rs3745274 (donor TT) + ABCB1 rs2235033 (recipient TC):** Increased nephrotoxicity risk (OR 7.5).
  - **CYP2B6 rs3745274 (donor TT) + SLCO1B1 rs2306283 (donor AG):** Increased de novo diabetes risk (OR 9.8).

Overall, the study suggests that both recipient and donor pharmacogenes—especially **CYP3A4, CYP3A5, CYP2C19, ABCB1, ABCC2, SLCO1B1, and CYP2B6**—are associated with tacrolimus exposure and long-term clinical outcomes after kidney transplantation.
