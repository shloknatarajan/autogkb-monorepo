## Summary

## Background
This study evaluated germline genetic predictors of **pegaspargase hypersensitivity** (grade 2 or higher) in children and adolescents with acute lymphoblastic leukemia treated on three frontline protocols (TXVI, AALL0232, AALL0434). The analysis included genome-wide SNP testing and HLA allele imputation/inference, with results analyzed within cohorts and then combined by meta-analysis, including ancestry-specific analyses.

## Key Findings
The strongest and most consistent pharmacogenomic signal was in the **class II HLA region**, especially among patients of **European ancestry**. The top HLA association was **HLA-DQB1*02:02** (**OR 2.14, P = 3.8×10^-9**), followed by **HLA-DRB1*07:01** (**OR 1.99, P = 2.2×10^-5**) and **HLA-DQA1*02:01** (**OR 1.99, P = 2.7×10^-5**). These alleles occurred on the same risk haplotype (**HLA-DRB1*07:01–DQA1*02:01–DQB1*02:02**). At the SNP level, all genome-wide significant variants in European-ancestry patients mapped to this same HLA class II region; the top SNP was **rs1694129** near **HLA-DQB1** (**OR 2.03, P = 1.1×10^-8**), which strongly tagged **HLA-DQB1*02:02** (r^2 = 0.96). In the full multi-ancestry analysis, the top SNP was **rs28383308** near **HLA-DQA1** (**OR 1.95, P = 4.9×10^-14**).

In **non-European ancestry** patients, the HLA risk alleles were less robust, but still showed **nominal** association in meta-analysis: **HLA-DRB1*07:01** (**OR 1.94, P = 0.011**), **HLA-DQA1*02:01** (**OR 2.09, P = 0.024**), and **HLA-DQB1*02:02** (**OR 1.75, P = 0.041**). The HLA-tagging SNP **rs1694129** also showed nominal association in non-European patients (**OR 2.01, P = 0.013**), although it was not significant when African-ancestry and Native American–ancestry groups were analyzed separately.

A notable non-HLA finding emerged in non-European patients: **rs9958628** in the **5′ UTR of ARHGAP28** was the strongest association with hypersensitivity (**OR 3.69, P = 8.9×10^-9**). It was also the top non-HLA signal in the overall cohort (**OR 3.03, P = 1.2×10^-7**) and was associated in the African-ancestry meta-analysis (**OR 2.03, P = 3.2×10^-5**). Two additional non-European signals near **GPBP1** also reached genome-wide significance: **rs79377225** (**OR 2.70, P = 2.5×10^-8**) and **rs11739459** (**OR 2.50, P = 2.7×10^-8**); both remained significant in trans-ancestry meta-analysis. Overall, the study shows that **HLA class II variants are the main inherited risk factors for pegaspargase hypersensitivity in European-ancestry patients**, while **ARHGAP28** and nearby non-HLA loci may contribute importantly in non-European populations.
