# Human Leukocyte Antigens and Sulfamethoxazole/Cotrimoxazole–Induced Severe Cutaneous Adverse Reactions: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis

## Summary

## Background
This systematic review and meta-analysis evaluated whether HLA genotypes are associated with severe cutaneous adverse reactions (SCARs) caused by sulfamethoxazole (SMX) or cotrimoxazole (CTX). The analysis included 6 case-control studies with 322 SCAR cases (236 SJS/TEN and 86 DRESS), compared against 8448 healthy controls and 229 SMX/CTX-tolerant controls. Outcomes were pooled as odds ratios (ORs) using random-effects models.

## Key Findings
Across all SCAR phenotypes, several HLA alleles were associated with higher risk of SMX/CTX-induced reactions. Compared with tolerant controls, significant associations were seen for **HLA-A*11:01** (OR 2.10, 95% CI 1.11-4.00), **HLA-B*13:01** (OR 5.96, 95% CI 1.58-22.56), **HLA-B*15:02** (OR 2.23, 95% CI 1.20-4.14), **HLA-B*38:02** (OR 3.47, 95% CI 1.42-8.48), and **HLA-C*08:01** (OR 2.63, 95% CI 1.07-6.44). Similar signals were seen versus healthy controls, including **HLA-A*11:01** (OR 2.16, p=.01), **HLA-B*13:01** (OR 5.35, p<.001), **HLA-B*15:02** (OR 1.87, p<.001), **HLA-B*38:02** (OR 3.21, p<.001), and **HLA-C*08:01** (OR 1.47, p=.04). Additional associations versus healthy controls were reported for **HLA-B*07:05** (OR 2.86, p=.002), **HLA-B*38:01** (OR 4.26), **HLA-C*03:04** (OR 3.33, p<.001), **HLA-C*04:06** (OR 3.28, p=.02), and **HLA-C*07:27** (OR 11.38).

Phenotype-specific analyses showed that **SJS/TEN** risk was most strongly linked to **HLA-B*15:02** and **HLA-B*38:02**. Against tolerant controls, **HLA-B*15:02** had an OR of 3.01 (95% CI 1.56-5.80; p=.001) and **HLA-B*38:02** an OR of 5.13 (95% CI 1.96-13.47). Against healthy controls, SJS/TEN was also associated with **HLA-B*13:01** (OR 2.49, p<.001), **HLA-B*07:05** (OR 2.80, p=.003), **HLA-B*38:01** (OR 4.26), **HLA-C*04:06** (OR 3.37, p=.03), **HLA-C*07:27** (OR 9.25), and **HLA-C*08:01** (OR 1.66, p=.01). For **DRESS**, the strongest associations were **HLA-B*13:01** (healthy controls: OR 23.93, p<.001; tolerant controls: OR 23.09, p=.002), **HLA-A*68:01** (healthy: OR 7.03; tolerant: OR 12.86), and **HLA-B*39:01** (healthy: OR 3.70, p=.002; tolerant: OR 4.56). Overall, **HLA-B*13:01** was the clearest allele associated with both major SCAR phenotypes, while **HLA-B*15:02** and **HLA-B*38:02** were more specific for SJS/TEN, and **HLA-A*68:01** and **HLA-B*39:01** were more specific for DRESS.
