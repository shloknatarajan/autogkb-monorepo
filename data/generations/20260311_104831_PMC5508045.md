# The impact of non-genetic and genetic factors on a stable warfarin dose in Thai patients

## Summary

## Background
This study evaluated how genetic variants affect stable warfarin dose requirements in 250 Thai adults receiving warfarin for atrial fibrillation, heart valve replacement, deep vein thrombosis, pulmonary embolism, or chronic thromboembolic pulmonary hypertension. Patients had stable anticoagulation (target INR 2.0–3.0), and four variants were genotyped: **VKORC1 -1639G>A (rs9923231)**, **CYP2C9*3 (rs1057910)**, **CYP4F2 rs2108622**, and **UGT1A1 rs887829**.

## Key Findings
- **Major genetic predictors of lower warfarin dose:** Variants in **VKORC1** and **CYP2C9** were strongly associated with needing less warfarin.
  - **VKORC1 -1639G>A (rs9923231):** Patients with variant genotypes required significantly lower stable weekly warfarin doses than those with **GG** (**p < 0.001**).
    - **GA vs GG:** mean dose **27.9 vs 38.6 mg/week**, about **27.7% lower**; multivariable model estimated **9.7 mg/week lower**.
    - **AA vs GG:** mean dose **19.4 vs 38.6 mg/week**, about **49.7% lower**; multivariable model estimated **18.1 mg/week lower**.
    - This was the strongest genetic contributor in the regression model, accounting for a large share of dose variability.
  - **CYP2C9*3 (rs1057910):** Carriers of the *3 allele also required significantly lower warfarin doses than **CYP2C9*1/*1** patients (**p = 0.006** in univariate analysis; **p < 0.001** in multivariable analysis).
    - **CYP2C9*1/*3 vs *1/*1:** mean dose **14.9 vs 22.6 mg/week**, about **34.1% lower**; multivariable model estimated **7.9 mg/week lower**.
    - **CYP2C9*3/*3 vs *1/*1:** mean dose **8.0 vs 22.6 mg/week**, about **64.6% lower**; multivariable model estimated **25.9 mg/week lower**.

- **Minor genetic predictor of higher warfarin dose:** **CYP4F2** showed a small effect that emerged after adjustment for clinical factors.
  - **CYP4F2 rs2108622:** In genotype-group comparisons, dose differences were **not significant** (**p = 0.172**).
    - However, in the multivariable model, the **TT genotype** was associated with a **higher** warfarin dose than **CC**, with an estimated increase of **4.1 mg/week** (**p = 0.016**).
    - Its contribution to overall dose variability was modest: **1.2%**.

- **Variant not associated with warfarin dose:** **UGT1A1 rs887829** did not affect stable warfarin dose in this cohort.
  - **UGT1A1 rs887829:** No significant difference in weekly warfarin dose across genotypes (**p = 0.921**).
    - Mean doses were similar across groups: **CC 22.0 mg/week**, **CT 23.0 mg/week**, **TT 22.8 mg/week**.

- **Combined pharmacogenomic model:** Genetic results were most informative when considered together with clinical factors.
  - **VKORC1 -1639G>A, CYP2C9*3, and CYP4F2 rs2108622**, together with age, BMI, antiplatelet use, amiodarone use, and smoking status, explained **51.3%** of the variability in stable warfarin dose.
  - Among the genetic factors, **VKORC1** had the largest effect, **CYP2C9*3** had a moderate effect, and **CYP4F2** had a small effect.
