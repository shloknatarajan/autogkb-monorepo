## Summary

## Background
This study analyzed pharmacogenomic predictors of efavirenz response in 606 treatment-naïve adults with HIV receiving once-daily efavirenz 400 mg or 600 mg plus tenofovir/emtricitabine, with outcomes assessed at 96 weeks. The investigators genotyped variants in **CYP2B6, CYP2A6, CYP3A4, NR1I3, NR1I2,** and **ABCB1** and tested associations with virologic suppression, adverse events, and efavirenz discontinuation using logistic and Cox regression models adjusted for key covariates.

## Key Findings
Overall, **none of the tested genetic variants were associated with virologic success** at week 96 (plasma HIV RNA <200 copies/mL). This included common efavirenz metabolism variants such as **CYP2B6 516G>T (rs3745274)**, **CYP2B6 983T>C (rs28399499)**, **CYP2B6 15582C>T (rs4803419)**, **CYP2A6*9B (rs8192726)**, **CYP2A6*17 (rs28399454)**, **ABCB1 3435C>T (rs1045642)**, **NR1I2 63396C>T (rs2472677)**, and others (all p > 0.05). Virologic suppression was high in both dose groups, and efavirenz PK measures were also not significantly associated with suppression at 96 weeks.

The clearest pharmacogenomic signals were for **toxicity and treatment discontinuation**, not efficacy. **CNS adverse events** were **less common** in carriers of **CYP2B6 983TC/CC** (OR 0.30, 95% CI 0.12–0.75, p = 0.010; abstract reports OR 0.35, p = 0.015), but **more common** in carriers of **CYP2B6 15582CT/TT** (OR 1.59, 95% CI 1.11–2.27, p = 0.011; abstract OR 1.46, p = 0.040) and **ABCB1 3435TT** (OR 2.14, 95% CI 1.25–3.67, p = 0.006; abstract OR 2.31, p = 0.003). For **overall efavirenz discontinuation**, risk was higher in patients with **CYP2B6 516GT** (HR 1.80, 95% CI 1.01–3.21, p = 0.047), **CYP2B6 516TT** (HR 2.66, 95% CI 1.26–5.60, p = 0.010), and **CYP2A6*9B CA/AA** (HR 2.00, 95% CI 1.14–3.52, p = 0.016), while **NR1I2 63396TT** was associated with a **lower** discontinuation risk (HR 0.22, 95% CI 0.07–0.67, p = 0.008).

Other variants, including **CYP3A4*22**, **NR1I3 540C>T**, **NR1I3 1089T>C**, **NR1I2 7635A>G**, and **CYP2A6*17**, were not significantly associated with virologic response in this study, and most were not retained as independent predictors of toxicity or discontinuation after adjustment. Importantly, the higher rate of stopping efavirenz because of adverse events was driven by **dose** rather than genotype, with **efavirenz 600 mg** showing greater risk than **400 mg** (OR 2.54, 95% CI 1.19–5.43, p = 0.016).
