# Effects of CYP2B6 G516T polymorphisms on plasma efavirenz and nevirapine levels when co-administered with rifampicin in HIV/TB co-infected Thai adults

## Summary

## Background
This study examined whether genetic variation in drug-metabolizing enzymes affects efavirenz or nevirapine exposure in 124 Thai adults with HIV/TB coinfection who were also receiving rifampicin. Patients received either efavirenz-based ART (n=65) or nevirapine-based ART (n=59). The investigators genotyped 7 SNPs in **CYP2B6** and **CYP3A4** using real-time PCR, but only **CYP2B6 516G>T (rs3745274)** had enough genotype variation for meaningful analysis of drug levels.

## Key Findings
The main pharmacogenomic signal was **CYP2B6 516TT**, which was strongly associated with higher **efavirenz** plasma concentrations. Mean 12-hour post-dose efavirenz concentrations in TT carriers were much higher than in GT or GG carriers at all measured time points: week 6 (**10.97 vs 3.43 vs 2.88 mg/L**), week 12 (**13.62 vs 3.35 vs 2.45 mg/L**), and 1 month after rifampicin stopped (**8.48 vs 3.21 vs 2.08 mg/L**); all comparisons were highly significant (**p < 0.0001**). This indicates that the TT genotype had a much larger effect on efavirenz exposure than rifampicin coadministration.

For **nevirapine**, **CYP2B6 516TT** was also associated with higher plasma levels, but the finding was weaker and only statistically significant at week 6 of ART. Mean 12-hour post-dose nevirapine concentrations for TT, GT, and GG were **14.09, 5.65, and 5.42 mg/L** at week 6 (**p = 0.003**), but differences were not significant at week 12 (**7.94, 5.58, 5.34 mg/L; p = 0.409**) or after rifampicin discontinuation (**9.44, 7.03, 6.43 mg/L; p = 0.448**). 

No clear pharmacogenomic association was shown for the other tested variants. Most **CYP2B6** and **CYP3A4** SNPs had little or no genotype variability in this cohort, so they could not be meaningfully analyzed. **CYP3A4 T878C** showed no interpretable association because the variant was too rare. Clinically, **CYP2B6 516TT** was **not significantly associated** with CD4 recovery or week-12 viral suppression, although there was a non-significant trend toward more patients with TT achieving HIV-1 RNA <50 copies/mL in both treatment groups.
