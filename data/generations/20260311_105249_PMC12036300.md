# The frequencies of CYP2C19*2, *3, and *17 alleles and their impact on the clinical efficacy of doubled maintenance dose of clopidogrel in Syrian patients with coronary artery disease

## Summary

## Background
This prospective cohort study evaluated whether **CYP2C19 genetic variation** affects outcomes in **Syrian patients with coronary artery disease undergoing PCI** who were treated with **dual antiplatelet therapy including a doubled maintenance dose of clopidogrel (150 mg/day)**. Genotyping was performed by **PCR and Sanger sequencing** in 50 patients, and clinical outcomes were followed for 18–24 months.

## Key Findings
- **Variant frequencies and metabolizer status:** The study first established how common key CYP2C19 alleles were in this Syrian PCI population.
  - **CYP2C19*2 (rs4244285):** Loss-of-function allele frequency was **8%**. Genotypes included **12% *1/*2** and **2% *2/*2**.
  - **CYP2C19*3 (rs4986893):** Not detected (**0%**).
  - **CYP2C19*17 (rs12248560):** Gain-of-function allele frequency was **17%**. Genotypes included **26% *1/*17** and **4% *17/*17**.
  - **Predicted CYP2C19 phenotype:** **56% normal metabolizers**, **12% intermediate metabolizers**, **26% rapid metabolizers**, **4% ultrarapid metabolizers**, and **2% poor metabolizers**.

- **Clopidogrel effectiveness outcomes:** In patients receiving the higher clopidogrel dose, the main loss-of-function variant was **not significantly associated with worse ischemic outcomes**.
  - **CYP2C19*2 / rs4244285:** No significant association with **refractory angina symptoms**: **33.3% in carriers vs 38.9% in non-carriers**, **P > 0.9999**.
  - **CYP2C19*2 / rs4244285:** No significant association with **major adverse cardiovascular events (MACE)**: **16.7% in carriers vs 2.9% in non-carriers**, **P = 0.2744**.
  - **CYP2C19*3 / rs4986893:** No outcome association could be assessed because this allele was **absent** in the cohort.

- **Bleeding risk during clopidogrel treatment:** Neither the gain-of-function nor loss-of-function CYP2C19 variant showed a statistically significant association with bleeding in this study.
  - **CYP2C19*17 / rs12248560:** Bleeding was numerically higher in carriers (**16.7%**) than non-carriers (**6.9%**), but this difference was **not significant** (**P = 0.567**).
  - **CYP2C19*2 / rs4244285:** Bleeding was also numerically higher in carriers (**33.3%**) than non-carriers (**5.7%**), but again this was **not significant** (**P = 0.0952**).

- **Other CYP2C19 alleles screened:** Additional alleles were checked but not observed in this population.
  - **CYP2C19*10 (rs6413438), *22 (rs140278421), *26 (rs375781227), *33 (rs370803989):** All were **not detected**. No drug-response associations were reported for these alleles.
